# Example Teacher Brief — Making Ten to Add

```text
lesson_title: Making Ten to Add
subject: Math
grade: 2
topic: make-ten addition
architecture: single_period_full
number_of_days: 1

big_idea: Making a ten helps students add more efficiently and explain their thinking.
essential_question: How can making a ten help us add quickly and explain our thinking?
learning_goals:
- I can use make-ten thinking to solve an addition problem.
- I can explain how I moved parts to make a ten.
- I can show my answer with pictures, words, or equations.

student_task: Students solve addition problems by making a ten first and explain their thinking.
final_product: Completed worksheet and short exit ticket response.
evidence_location: exit_ticket

required_outputs:
- teacher_guide
- slides
- worksheet
- exit_ticket

teacher_constraints:
- teacher notes stay teacher-only
- student-facing language stays concrete and short

student_supports:
- anchor reminders stay embedded in worksheet
- visual and sentence-level support should be student-facing

timing_or_day_flow:
- warm-up
- teacher model
- guided practice
- independent worksheet
- exit ticket

notes:
- keep cognitive load low
- avoid unnecessary vocabulary overload
```

## Why this example is canonical

This example shows a standard `single_period_full` lesson where:
- final evidence is short and separate
- the output set is compact
- teacher guidance and student work remain distinct
