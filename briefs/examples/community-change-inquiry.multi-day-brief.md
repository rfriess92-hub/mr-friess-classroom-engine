# Example Teacher Brief — Community Change Inquiry

```text
lesson_title: Community Change Inquiry
subject: English Language Arts
grade: 8
topic: community change inquiry
architecture: multi_day_sequence
number_of_days: 2

big_idea: Students gather evidence over time, then use it to produce a final written response.
essential_question: How can evidence help us explain a change that matters to our community?
learning_goals:
- I can gather relevant evidence across two days.
- I can use a checkpoint to improve my thinking before final writing.
- I can place final evidence in the declared final response artifact.

student_task: Students investigate a community change question, gather evidence, and prepare for a final written response.
final_product: Final written response based on gathered evidence.
evidence_location: final_response_sheet

required_outputs:
- lesson_overview
- teacher_guide
- slides
- task_sheet
- checkpoint_sheet
- final_response_sheet

teacher_constraints:
- teacher-only checkpoint remains teacher-facing
- final response is not released before checkpoint
- embedded supports remain embedded in student task materials

student_supports:
- evidence-gathering supports in task sheet
- planning reminders in final response sheet

timing_or_day_flow:
- Day 1: launch, gather evidence, checkpoint
- Day 2: continue evidence, write final response

notes:
- preserve carryover from Day 1 to Day 2
```

## Why this example is canonical

This example shows a standard `multi_day_sequence` lesson where:
- continuity across days matters
- teacher-controlled checkpoint/release matters
- final evidence lives in a distinct final response artifact
