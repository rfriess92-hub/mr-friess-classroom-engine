# Grade 8 Bias and Decision-Making — Teacher Lesson Brief

lesson_title: Bias and Decision-Making
subject: Careers
grade: 8
topic: Bias and decision-making
architecture: multi_day_sequence
number_of_days: 5

big_idea: Bias shapes what students notice, believe, and decide in school life, social media, friendships, online recommendations, and future choices. Awareness helps students slow down quick judgments and make fairer decisions.
essential_question: How can bias shape what we notice, believe, and decide, and how can we become more aware of it?
learning_goals:
- identify different types of bias, including stereotypes, framing, and cognitive biases
- analyze how media and recommendations can influence thinking
- apply a practical pause routine before judging, sharing, or deciding
- connect bias awareness to real Grade 8 life without making the unit only about careers

student_task: Students complete short daily bias thinking tasks, analyze examples and scenarios, and build a personal bias pause routine.
final_product: Day 5 exit ticket reflection explaining one kind of bias and one practical awareness strategy.
evidence_location: exit_ticket

required_outputs:
- lesson_overview
- teacher_guide
- slides
- task_sheet
- exit_ticket

teacher_constraints:
- keep the unit broader than careers while allowing light careers connections
- preserve teacher/student separation
- keep examples age-appropriate and relevant to Grade 8 life
- include differentiated student versions
- avoid forcing personal disclosure

student_supports:
- embedded cue words and reflection supports
- differentiated variants with shared learning target
- practical, low-risk examples from school, media, online life, and group work

timing_or_day_flow:
- 79 minute blocks most days
- 55 minute Wednesday
- each day includes roughly 20 to 30 minutes of bias lesson work plus separate project work time

notes:
- user wants differentiated daily student versions and a matching slide deck
- user wants Mr. Friess on all classroom-facing documents
- user prefers each day as its own student handout rather than a combined student workbook
