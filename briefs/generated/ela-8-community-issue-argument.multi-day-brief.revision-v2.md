# Generated Teacher Brief — ELA 8 Community Issue Argument (Revision v2)

```text
lesson_title: Writing About a Community Issue
subject: English Language Arts
grade: 8
topic: community issue argument
architecture: multi_day_sequence
number_of_days: 2

big_idea: Students write a stronger argument when they use a real evidence set, explain how evidence supports their reasons, and move from planning to final writing through a clear checkpoint and release structure.
essential_question: How can we use reasons and evidence to write a clear argument about a community issue?
learning_goals:
- I can identify a community issue and explain why it matters.
- I can choose reasons that support my opinion.
- I can use evidence and explanation to strengthen an argument.
- I can write a clear argument paragraph using planning from an earlier stage.

student_task: Students examine a familiar school issue, read a short evidence set, collect reasons and evidence on Day 1, strengthen their thinking through a teacher checkpoint, and write a final argument paragraph on Day 2.
final_product: Final argument paragraph supported by planning evidence.
evidence_location: final_response_sheet

required_outputs:
- lesson_overview
- teacher_guide
- slides
- task_sheet
- checkpoint_sheet
- final_response_sheet

teacher_constraints:
- teacher notes stay teacher-only
- the checkpoint remains teacher-facing
- the final response sheet is not released before the checkpoint
- planning work stays in the task sheet and final evidence stays in the final response sheet
- student-facing language should stay clear and concrete
- slides should show instructional models and source material, not teacher-operating language

student_supports:
- sentence starters remain embedded in the task sheet and final response sheet
- argument prompts should keep students focused on reasons, evidence, and explanation
- carryover from Day 1 planning to Day 2 writing should remain explicit
- students should see at least one worked model of claim + reason + evidence + explanation

timing_or_day_flow:
- Day 1: introduce the issue, study the evidence set, gather reasons and evidence, complete checkpoint
- Day 2: revisit planning, strengthen explanation, write final argument paragraph

notes:
- use a familiar school issue so students can access the task quickly
- the evidence set should be short, readable, and usable in one class period
- include one worked model and one weak-vs-strong revision example
```
