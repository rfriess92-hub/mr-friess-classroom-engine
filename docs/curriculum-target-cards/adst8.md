# ADST 8 Target Cards

Use these cards to start future ADST 8 packages from design or technology target and evidence before context.

## 1. Design Thinking Process

Students need to identify need, criteria, constraints, ideas, feedback, revision, and final reflection.

Best evidence: design brief, idea sketches, feedback note, revision reflection.

Support moves: design brief frame, fewer ideas required, check-in boxes, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: design/build, garden systems, digital tools, community problems, entrepreneurship.

## 2. Technical Drawing and Drafting

Students need to use labels, dimensions, views, scale, and clear communication in a technical plan.

Best evidence: top-view drawing, side/front view, checklist, explanation.

Support moves: fixed dimensions, pre-started grid, fewer labels, top-view-only option.

Possible contexts: design/build/trades, scale drawings, woodwork, garden design.

## 3. Tool, Workspace, and Digital Safety

Students need to identify hazards, follow procedures, use tools responsibly, and explain safe choices.

Best evidence: safety checklist, hazard sort, scenario response.

Support moves: visual checklist, repeated routines, one tool at a time, oral explanation.

Possible contexts: shops, food studies, robotics, digital spaces, outdoor tasks.

## 4. Digital Literacy and Source Checking

Students need to check source reliability, purpose, author, date, and bias before using information.

Best evidence: source checklist, comparison organizer, short source-use explanation.

Support moves: teacher-selected source set, checklist, limited source count, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: digital/media, Careers, science research, consumer information.

## 5. Computational Thinking and Coding Logic

Students need to break problems into steps, identify patterns, use algorithms, debug, and explain logic.

Best evidence: flowchart, pseudocode, debug notes, simple program/task output.

Support moves: unplugged tasks, flowchart first, partial code, debug checklist.

Possible contexts: digital/coding/data, robotics, data tracking, games.

## 6. Food Studies and Local Food Systems

Students need to understand basic food preparation, safety, measurement, local food systems, and decision-making.

Best evidence: recipe/measurement task, safety checklist, food system reflection.

Support moves: visual recipe steps, friendly fractions, allergy/sensitivity awareness, no personal disclosure.

Possible contexts: garden/food systems, health choices, local community, consumer/money.

## 7. Entrepreneurship and Marketing

Students need to identify a need, design a product/service idea, consider audience, cost, and message.

Best evidence: mini business plan, ad/media draft, cost comparison, pitch.

Support moves: product idea bank, simple cost table, sentence frames, two-choice decisions.

Possible contexts: consumer/money, community work, media arts, design/build.

## 8. Media Arts and Visual Communication

Students need to create a visual message for an audience using layout, image, text, and purpose.

Best evidence: design plan, draft, peer feedback, reflection.

Support moves: template, design checklist, limited elements, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: digital/media, community campaigns, Careers, ELA media literacy.

## 9. Woodwork / Materials / Making

Students need to understand materials, measurement, planning, safety, and reflection in a making task.

Best evidence: plan, measurement sheet, safety check, process reflection.

Support moves: fixed dimensions, pre-cut or simulated option, safety checklist, teacher check-ins.

Possible contexts: design/build/trades, garden structures, technical drawing.

## 10. Robotics / Sensors / Automated Systems

Students need to understand input/output, sensors, simple automation logic, testing, and debugging.

Best evidence: system diagram, test log, algorithm or flowchart, reflection.

Support moves: unplugged model, labelled diagram, partial flowchart, test checklist.

Possible contexts: digital/coding/data, garden sensors, trades/automation, science data.

## 11. ADST Skills Recovery Pack

Students need to rebuild planning, safety, measurement, digital organization, feedback, and reflection routines.

Best evidence: checklist use, design mini-task, reflection, skill tracker.

Support moves: predictable steps, short tasks, visual checklist, role cards, teacher check-ins.

Possible contexts: low-risk, low-material ADST examples.
