# ELA 8 Target Cards

Use these cards to start future ELA 8 packages from literacy target and evidence before context.

## 1. Reading Strategies for Short Non-Fiction

Students need to preview, annotate, identify main idea, find evidence, and explain meaning.

Best evidence: annotated text, main idea/evidence organizer, short response.

Support moves: shorter text, chunked reading, highlighted sections, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: community, environment, health/media, consumer issues, careers.

## 2. Paragraph Writing Foundations

Students need to write a focused paragraph with topic sentence, evidence/details, explanation, and closing.

Best evidence: paragraph planner, draft, revision checklist.

Support moves: paragraph frame, sentence starters, reduced length, oral rehearsal.

Possible contexts: any subject with low-cognitive-load topics.

## 3. Summary Skills

Students need to identify main ideas, leave out small details, and write a concise summary in their own words.

Best evidence: main idea organizer, summary draft, self-check.

Support moves: who/what/why frame, word limit, chunked text, key sentence prompts.

Possible contexts: non-fiction, careers, science, local articles, digital media.

## 4. Opinion and Argument with Evidence

Students need to make a claim, support it with evidence, explain reasoning, and address audience.

Best evidence: claim-evidence-reasoning organizer, paragraph or short argument.

Support moves: two evidence options, sentence frames, claim bank, oral rehearsal.

Possible contexts: community issues, consumer/media, environment, school decisions.

## 5. Personal Narrative and Growth Writing

Students need to tell a focused story, show change or learning, and use details.

Best evidence: story map, narrative draft, reflection.

Support moves: fictional option, low-disclosure choice, timeline frame, sentence starters.

Possible contexts: stories/identity/growth, Careers, belonging, goals.

## 6. Research Basics and Source Use

Students need to ask a question, gather basic information, record source details, and use notes responsibly.

Best evidence: research organizer, source notes, short product.

Support moves: teacher-selected sources, note frames, source checklist, limited source count.

Possible contexts: Careers, local work, digital citizenship, science topics, community.

## 7. Visual and Media Literacy

Students need to read images/media, identify message/audience, notice techniques, and explain effect.

Best evidence: media analysis organizer, short response, discussion notes.

Support moves: guided questions, vocabulary bank, one image/ad at a time.

Possible contexts: consumer/money, health/media, digital citizenship, community.

## 8. Oral Communication and Discussion Skills

Students need to prepare ideas, listen, ask questions, and respond respectfully.

Best evidence: discussion prep sheet, note-catcher, reflection.

Support moves: roles, sentence stems, partner rehearsal, opt-in oral recording.

Possible contexts: Careers, ELA texts, science decisions, community issues.

## 9. Literary Response: Identity, Place, and Growth

Students need to respond to character, setting, theme, identity, and change using evidence.

Best evidence: response organizer, paragraph, discussion notes.

Support moves: short excerpts, evidence choices, sentence frames, visual organizer.

Possible contexts: stories/identity/growth, place, belonging, local/community.

## 10. First Peoples Text Protocol-Aware Response

Students need to respond respectfully to teacher-selected/local-approved First Peoples texts, noticing voice, place, theme, and responsibility.

Best evidence: protocol-aware response organizer, reflection, discussion preparation.

Support moves: teacher-selected approved sources, clear protocol notes, no invented stories, respectful sentence frames.

Possible contexts: teacher-approved/local sources, place-based learning, identity, land, community.

Important note: do not invent generic First Peoples stories or knowledge.

## 11. ELA Skills Recovery Pack

Students need to rebuild reading stamina, sentence clarity, vocabulary, evidence use, and paragraph structure.

Best evidence: short reading responses, sentence practice, vocabulary organizer, paragraph frame.

Support moves: short texts, chunked tasks, sentence frames, oral rehearsal, reduced copying.

Possible contexts: high-interest, low-barrier topics.
