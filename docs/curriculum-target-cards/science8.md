# Science 8 Target Cards

Use these cards to start future Science 8 packages from target and evidence before context.

## 1. Scientific Inquiry, Observation, and CER

Students need to make observations, ask questions, collect evidence, and write claim-evidence-reasoning responses.

Best evidence: observation notes, data table, CER organizer, short conclusion.

Support moves: sentence frames, word bank, fewer observations, teacher check-ins.

Possible contexts: outdoor place, garden systems, health/body, local environment, digital/data.

## 2. Characteristics of Life and Cell Theory

Students need to describe characteristics of living things and explain cells as basic units of life.

Best evidence: classification table, labelled notes, explanation frame.

Support moves: picture sort, word bank, two-column organizer, oral explanation.

Possible contexts: garden/food systems, outdoor environment, health/body, digital models.

## 3. Plant and Animal Cells

Students need to identify major cell parts, compare plant and animal cells, and explain structure-function connections.

Best evidence: labelled cell diagram, comparison table, function explanation.

Support moves: pre-labelled diagrams, limited organelle set, matching bank, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: garden systems, health/body, digital models, food systems.

## 4. Photosynthesis and Cellular Respiration

Students need to describe inputs and outputs and explain how energy and matter move through living systems.

Best evidence: flow diagram, input/output table, short explanation.

Support moves: flow arrows, word bank, sentence frames, reduced equation load.

Possible contexts: garden/food systems, outdoor environment, health/body, local sustainability.

## 5. Microorganisms and Decomposers

Students need to explain roles of microorganisms and decomposers in ecosystems, food systems, and health.

Best evidence: role chart, cause-effect explanation, case example.

Support moves: helpful/harmful sort, image prompts, reduced reading, sentence starters.

Possible contexts: compost, food systems, health/body, outdoor environment.

## 6. Matter and Particle Model

Students need to describe states of matter and explain changes using particles.

Best evidence: particle diagrams, explanation of change, observation notes.

Support moves: diagram frames, vocabulary bank, concrete demos, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: food studies, weather, materials, trades, local environment.

## 7. Atomic Theory, Elements, and Compounds

Students need to distinguish atoms, elements, compounds, and mixtures using models and examples.

Best evidence: classification table, model drawings, explanation.

Support moves: limited examples, card sort, pre-drawn models, word bank.

Possible contexts: materials, food labels, environment, health/body.

## 8. Light, Waves, and Electromagnetic Spectrum

Students need to describe light as waves, compare parts of the spectrum, and connect properties to uses.

Best evidence: spectrum organizer, use-case explanation, labelled diagram.

Support moves: visual spectrum, matching table, reduced terms, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: health/body, digital/media, outdoor environment, careers.

## 9. Immune System and Disease Basics

Students need to describe basic immune responses, barriers, pathogens, and prevention choices.

Best evidence: cause-effect organizer, vocabulary match, short decision explanation.

Support moves: simple sequence organizer, neutral examples, word bank, no personal disclosure.

Possible contexts: health/body/choices, media literacy, community health, digital/source checking.

## 10. Plate Tectonics, Earth Processes, and Local Geology

Students need to explain plate movement, landforms, earthquakes/volcanoes, and local geological evidence.

Best evidence: labelled diagram, map notes, explanation of process.

Support moves: simplified diagrams, local photo prompts, vocabulary cards, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: Kamloops/local geology, outdoor place, maps/data, community planning.

## 11. Science Skills Recovery and Vocabulary Support

Students need to rebuild science vocabulary, observation, classification, diagram reading, and explanation skills.

Best evidence: vocabulary organizer, labelled diagram, observation notes, short explanation.

Support moves: word banks, visuals, matching, chunked notes, oral rehearsal.

Possible contexts: any low-reading-load science context.
