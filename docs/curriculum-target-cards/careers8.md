# Career Education 8 Target Cards

Use these cards to start future Careers 8 packages from target and evidence before context. Avoid forced personal disclosure.

## 1. Values, Skills, Interests, and Strengths

Students need to identify personal strengths, interests, values, and possible connections to learning or work.

Best evidence: self-assessment organizer, reflection, goal note.

Support moves: choice bank, private reflection option, sentence frames, strengths list.

Possible contexts: stories/identity/growth, local work, guest speakers, digital portfolios.

## 2. Goal Setting and Reflection

Students need to set a realistic goal, choose steps, monitor progress, and reflect.

Best evidence: goal planner, checkpoint log, reflection.

Support moves: SMART-ish frame, short timeline, teacher check-ins, examples.

Possible contexts: school skills, health choices, projects, Careers, ADST.

## 3. Guest Speaker Prep and Reflection

Students need to prepare questions, take notes, connect speaker ideas to skills/pathways, and reflect.

Best evidence: note-catcher, question list, reflection.

Support moves: question stems, three-column note-catcher, word bank, oral reflection.

Possible contexts: local work, Kamloops community, trades, health, digital careers.

## 4. Local Career Pathways

Students need to explore local jobs/pathways, identify skills/training, and connect to interests.

Best evidence: career research organizer, pathway map, short explanation.

Support moves: teacher-selected sources, limited fields, local examples, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: Kamloops/local work, trades, health, digital careers, environment.

## 5. Workplace Skills and Employability

Students need to identify communication, teamwork, reliability, problem-solving, and safety skills.

Best evidence: workplace skills chart, scenario response, reflection.

Support moves: scenario cards, skill word bank, matching task, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: trades, food systems, local work, entrepreneurship, group projects.

## 6. Workplace Safety Basics

Students need to recognize hazards, follow safety routines, and explain safe choices.

Best evidence: safety checklist, scenario response, hazard ID task.

Support moves: visual hazard sort, simple checklists, oral explanation, repeated routines.

Possible contexts: ADST shops, food studies, digital safety, outdoor work, labs.

## 7. Digital Identity and First Impressions

Students need to understand how online choices shape first impressions and future opportunities.

Best evidence: digital footprint reflection, scenario analysis, action plan.

Support moves: fictional scenarios, no personal account disclosure, checklist, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: digital citizenship, Careers, media literacy, community.

## 8. Project Management and Collaboration

Students need to plan tasks, assign roles, track progress, communicate, and reflect on collaboration.

Best evidence: project plan, role chart, progress tracker, group reflection.

Support moves: role cards, checklist, short checkpoints, teacher check-in boxes.

Possible contexts: ADST projects, ELA presentations, science investigations, community work.

## 9. Volunteering and Community Contribution

Students need to understand contribution, identify community needs, and reflect on ways to help.

Best evidence: community contribution plan, reflection, interview/question prep.

Support moves: local example list, small-action options, sentence frames.

Possible contexts: Kamloops/local work, school community, environment, stories/identity.

## 10. Education and Training Pathways

Students need to connect careers to courses, training, skills, and possible next steps.

Best evidence: pathway organizer, comparison table, reflection.

Support moves: simplified pathway map, teacher-selected examples, vocabulary bank.

Possible contexts: local careers, trades, digital careers, health, environment.

## 11. Careers Skills Recovery Pack

Students need to rebuild self-reflection, communication, goal-setting, organization, and career vocabulary.

Best evidence: short reflections, skill checklists, goal planner, note-catcher.

Support moves: choice banks, sentence frames, oral explanation, reduced writing.

Possible contexts: low-barrier Careers examples.
