# Printable Voice Trial Packet

## Purpose

This packet gives the teacher a print-ready set for the Voice Trial arc.

Voice = Fluency.

Students practise reading with more control through modelling, rereading, phrasing, punctuation, and self-correction.

This packet is for classroom use. It is not a public performance packet.

## Packet contents

1. Teacher cover page
2. Student arc overview
3. Voice strategy reference
4. Quest 1: Echo Reading Quest
5. Quest 2: Second Read Quest
6. Quest 3: Phrase Builder Quest
7. Quest 4: Error Recovery Quest
8. Voice Boss Quest
9. Safe Reread Route
10. Teacher tracker

# 1. Teacher Cover Page

## Arc purpose

Voice Trial builds safe fluency routines.

Students practise:

- echo reading
- rereading
- phrase chunking
- punctuation use
- self-correction
- reflection on what improved

## Teacher stance

Voice work must stay safe.

Do not use surprise public reading, speed contests, or public comparison.

Progress information stays private.

## Best use

Use this packet after students know:

- Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft
- the core loop
- Guild Passport basics
- solo evidence expectations
- party work does not replace individual evidence

## Arc completion rule

A student completes the arc when they have:

- practised required Voice strategies
- submitted clear Voice evidence
- reflected on growth
- completed or conferenced around the Voice Boss Quest
- chosen a next Voice goal

# 2. Student Arc Overview

## Welcome to Voice Trial

Voice Trial is about reading with more control.

You are training fluency. That means reading more smoothly, accurately, and confidently.

You are not being judged against other students.

Your goal is to show evidence that a strategy helped your reading improve from your own starting point.

## Voice strategies

| Strategy | What it means |
|---|---|
| Echo Cast | I repeat a model read. |
| Second Read Surge | I improve on a reread. |
| Punctuation Shield | I use punctuation to guide phrasing. |
| Error Recovery | I notice and fix a reading error. |
| Confident Voice | I explain a fluency strategy. |

## Required quests

| Quest | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Echo Reading Quest | reread reflection |
| Second Read Quest | first/second read comparison |
| Phrase Builder Quest | marked text or phrasing reflection |
| Error Recovery Quest | self-correction note |
| Voice Boss Quest | recording, conference, or written reflection |

# 3. Voice Strategy Reference

## Echo Cast

Listen to a model read.

Repeat a short part.

Notice what became smoother.

## Second Read Surge

Read once.

Read again.

Notice what improved.

## Punctuation Shield

Use commas, periods, question marks, and line breaks to help phrasing.

Do not rush through punctuation.

## Error Recovery

If something sounds wrong or does not make sense, stop and repair.

Try:

- rereading the sentence
- checking the word again
- slowing down
- using the meaning of the sentence
- asking for support

## Confident Voice

Name the strategy you used.

Explain how it helped.

Set your next Voice goal.
