# Printable Launch Packet

## Purpose

This packet gives the teacher a simple printable launch set for the first two weeks of the Literacy Guild.

It combines the launch routine, student overview, first evidence tasks, first ability cards, and first party roles into one classroom-ready packet.

Print only what you need. Do not hand out every page at once.

## Packet contents

1. Teacher launch cover
2. Student overview handout
3. Three paths reference
4. Guild Passport starter page
5. Character quickstart
6. First ability cards
7. First party role cards
8. Week 1 evidence slips
9. Week 2 evidence slips
10. Teacher launch tracker

---

# 1. Teacher Launch Cover

## Launch rule

Routines first. Mechanics second. Lore third.

If students are confused, simplify the game layer and protect the reading routine.

## Core student message

This class helps you train reading skills. We track growth privately and collect evidence of what is getting stronger.

## Week 1 goals

- establish safety and dignity
- introduce Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft
- teach the daily routine
- start Guild Passports
- collect private starting information
- complete one low-stakes evidence task

## Week 2 goals

- review the three paths
- start or refine character sheets
- use first ability cards
- practise simple party roles
- submit clean solo evidence
- log evidence without too much teacher time

## Do not do during launch

- do not post public rankings
- do not compare reading levels
- do not introduce every mechanic
- do not overplay fantasy language
- do not let party work replace solo evidence

# 2. Student Overview Handout

## Welcome to the Literacy Guild

This is a reading training course.

The game layer gives the class structure. The real goal is to build stronger reading strategies, collect evidence of growth, and know your next move.

## The three paths

| Path | Reading skill | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Voice | Fluency | I can read more smoothly, accurately, and confidently. |
| Insight | Comprehension | I can understand, explain, and prove my thinking. |
| Wordcraft | Vocabulary | I can unlock, understand, and use important words. |

You may focus on one path at a time, but you will train all three.

## The Guild

The whole class is the Guild.

The Guild is the learning community. We work toward shared goals, but students are not ranked against one another.

## Parties

A party is a small group inside the Guild.

Parties help with discussion, rereading, word work, rehearsal, evidence checking, and preparation for solo work.

Party work helps you train. It does not replace your own evidence.

## Your character

Your character shows how you train, which strategies you use, what evidence you collect, and how you grow over time.

Your character is not your reading level.

## Core loop

Train -> Attempt -> Feedback -> Revise -> Log Evidence -> Level Up

## Evidence

Evidence is proof that you used a strategy or grew in a skill.

Evidence can be a written answer, revised answer, gist statement, word strategy explanation, reread reflection, recording, conference note, or Character Log entry.

Evidence does not need to be long. It needs to be clear.

# 3. Three Paths Reference

## Voice = Fluency

Voice is about reading with more control.

You might show Voice evidence by:

- rereading a short part
- marking phrase chunks
- noticing smoother reading
- explaining how punctuation helped
- recording or conferencing privately

## Insight = Comprehension

Insight is about understanding and proving your thinking.

You might show Insight evidence by:

- writing a gist statement
- revising a gist
- answering with text evidence
- making an inference
- naming a repair strategy

## Wordcraft = Vocabulary

Wordcraft is about unlocking and using important words.

You might show Wordcraft evidence by:

- using context clues
- breaking down word parts
- explaining a target word
- using a word accurately
- building a word map

# 6. First Ability Cards

Cut apart if useful.

## Echo Cast

Path: Voice

Strategy: Repeat a model read to build fluency.

Evidence I can show:

- reread note
- marked phrase
- reflection on what got smoother

## Gist Map

Path: Insight

Strategy: Say what a section is mostly about.

Evidence I can show:

- first gist
- revised gist
- explanation of what changed

## Context Torch

Path: Wordcraft

Strategy: Use nearby words to infer meaning.

Evidence I can show:

- target word
- clue words
- smart guess
- explanation of why the guess fits

## End-of-launch teacher check

Answer briefly:

1. Can students explain Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft?
2. Can students submit solo evidence?
3. Did party roles help or distract?
4. Did any game mechanic create confusion or comparison?
5. Who needs teacher-table support next?
6. Is the class ready for Voice Trial, or should Three-Path Training repeat?

## Simplification rule

If launch feels too complicated, protect these three things:

1. the daily routine
2. the three paths
3. solo evidence
