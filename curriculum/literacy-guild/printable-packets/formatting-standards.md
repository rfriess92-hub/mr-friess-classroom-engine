# Printable Packet Formatting Standards

## Purpose

Use this standard for all Literacy Guild printable packets.

The goal is to make materials readable, printable, and usable for students who need reading support, including dyslexic students.

## Approved student formatting

- Font: Calibri
- Body text size: 14 pt
- Line spacing: 1.2
- Alignment: left aligned only
- Paragraphs: short, with clear space between ideas
- Page layout: enough white space for tracking and handwriting
- Response areas: wide answer lines, not boxed response areas
- Headings: clear, predictable, and not decorative

## Approved teacher formatting

- Font: Calibri
- Body text size: 12 pt
- Line spacing: 1.2
- Alignment: left aligned only
- Tables are acceptable for trackers and teacher notes.

## Avoid

- justified text
- italics
- decorative fonts
- fantasy-style fonts
- crowded tables on student pages
- long paragraphs
- too many game terms on one page
- all-caps headings except short copy labels
- relying on colour alone for meaning
- public comparison language
- boxed answer areas unless there is a specific reason

## Tone model

Use this tone for student-facing writing:

`Your character is a way to track how you practise, what strategies you try, and what gets stronger.`

This tone is direct, calm, and clear. It sounds like a teacher in the room, not a product description.

## Student prompt pattern

Use predictable prompts:

- What I worked on:
- The strategy I tried:
- What changed:
- What helped:
- My next step:

Use sentence starters when students need support:

- At first, I thought...
- Now I think...
- The clue that helped was...
- The part I fixed was...
- The thing that helped was...
- Next, I need to practise...

Use prompt plus starter. Do not repeat the same wording twice.

Example:

`Why did you pick this path?`

`I picked this because...`

## Teacher/student separation

Label pages clearly:

- Teacher Copy
- Student Copy

Teacher pages can include rationale, setup notes, and tracking.

Student pages should focus on the move students need to make that day.

## Game language rule

Reading comes first.

Use game terms only when they help students understand the routine.

Do not introduce too many terms at launch.

For the launch packet, students only need:

- Voice
- Insight
- Wordcraft
- strategy
- evidence
- goal

Other terms can be introduced later when students are ready.

## Printing notes

If available, print student pages on off-white paper to reduce glare.

Cards may be cut out or kept as reference pages.

Evidence slips should be printable one day at a time.

Teacher trackers should be printable separately from student pages.
