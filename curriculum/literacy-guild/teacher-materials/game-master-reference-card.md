# Game Master Reference Card

## Purpose

This is the teacher quick-reference card for running the Literacy Guild.

Use it to keep the game layer clear, useful, and instructionally grounded.

## Your role

You are the Game Master, also called the Guildmaster if you want a more classroom-specific title.

You do not compete with students. You run the world, protect the rules, teach the strategies, and confirm evidence.

## Core promise

Students are not ranked against one another.

The game tracks training, evidence, strategy use, reflection, and growth.

## Core loop

Train -> Attempt -> Feedback -> Revise -> Log Evidence -> Level Up

Ask daily:

- What skill are we training?
- What strategy are students using?
- What evidence will they collect?
- What feedback or revision happens?
- What gets logged?

## Game Master abilities

| Ability | Use it when... | Teacher move |
|---|---|---|
| Quest Call | Starting a lesson or week | Name the challenge, path, and target. |
| Strategy Reveal | Students need a tool | Model the strategy before practice. |
| Evidence Check | Students submit work | Confirm whether evidence is clear enough. |
| Reroute | The task is not working | Adjust the path without blaming students. |
| Safe Table | A group needs support | Pull a small group for teacher-table instruction. |
| Pause the Realm | Game language is distracting | Stop the game layer and reteach the real skill. |
| Level Gate | A rank-up is requested | Approve, delay, or name the next evidence needed. |
| Private Counsel | A student needs feedback | Hold a quiet progress conference. |
| Shield of Dignity | Comparison appears | Protect privacy and reset the norm. |

## Before class checklist

- What is the main path today?
- What text or task are we using?
- What strategy will I model?
- What evidence will students produce?
- What supports are allowed?
- Which students need teacher-table attention?
- Which XP actions will count today?

## During class checklist

- Keep the target visible.
- Use game language briefly.
- Teach the real strategy explicitly.
- Watch for confusion.
- Protect solo evidence time.
- Award XP in simple batches.
- Do not let party work replace individual evidence.

## After class checklist

- Record only useful notes.
- Mark who submitted evidence.
- Identify conference needs.
- Confirm or delay rank-ups.
- Decide the next teacher-table focus.

## Quick scripts

### Launching a quest

Today your quest is to train one clear skill. The game part is the structure. The real goal is evidence that your reading is getting stronger.

### Approving a rank-up

You have evidence for this rank because you used the strategy and can explain what it did for your reading.

### Delaying a rank-up

Not yet. You are training the right skill, but I need clearer evidence before I confirm the rank.

### Resetting comparison

We do not rank readers against each other in this class. Your progress data is private. We compare your evidence to your own next goal.

### Simplifying the game

We are pausing the game layer for a minute. The skill matters more than the mechanics.

## Do not do these

- Do not make DIBELS or progress scores public.
- Do not equate character level with reading level.
- Do not award points for everything.
- Do not let confident students dominate parties.
- Do not force public oral reading before routines are safe.
- Do not keep mechanics that make instruction slower.

## Good signs

The system is working when:

- students can name the path they are training
- students know what evidence they need
- students revise without seeing it as punishment
- conferences are easier
- the tracker changes instruction
- students describe growth from their own baseline

## Simplification rule

If the game layer gets in the way, cut the game layer first. Keep the reading routine.
