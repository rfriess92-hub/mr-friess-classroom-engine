# Week 1 Launch Plan

## Purpose

Week 1 introduces the Literacy Guild without overloading students.

Main goals:

- establish safety and dignity
- introduce Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft
- teach the daily routine
- create Guild Passports
- collect private starting information
- complete one low-stakes evidence task

## Teacher stance

This is a training space. Students are not being ranked against one another.

The week should feel structured, mature, and calm. Do not overplay the fantasy layer.

## Core student message

This class helps you train reading skills. We will track your growth privately and build evidence of what is getting stronger.

## Day 1: Enter the Guild

### Goals

- introduce the course frame
- explain the three paths
- set dignity norms
- begin the Guild Passport

### Flow

1. Entry prompt: What makes reading harder or easier?
2. Teacher introduction: This is a training model.
3. Introduce the three paths: Voice, Insight, Wordcraft.
4. Explain what the system is not: not a public ranking, not a reading-level label.
5. Start Guild Passport setup.
6. Exit ticket: Which path do you think you most want to train first?

### Evidence

- exit ticket
- passport started

## Day 2: Learn the Loop

### Goals

- teach the core loop
- practise a short, low-risk task
- introduce XP as training credit

### Flow

1. Daily Encounter: short sentence or word puzzle.
2. Teach the loop: Train -> Attempt -> Feedback -> Revise -> Log Evidence -> Level Up.
3. Model one small reading task.
4. Students attempt and revise.
5. Students log one piece of evidence.
6. Exit reflection: What changed from first attempt to second attempt?

### Evidence

- revised task
- short reflection

## Day 3: Voice Training

### Goals

- introduce Voice as fluency
- practise rereading safely
- avoid public performance pressure

### Flow

1. Daily Encounter: punctuation or phrase chunking.
2. Teacher models a short read and reread.
3. Students practise echo or partner rereading.
4. Students mark one place where phrasing improved.
5. Character Log: Voice evidence entry.

### Evidence

- reread note
- Voice reflection

## Day 4: Insight and Wordcraft Training

### Goals

- introduce gist and key words
- connect comprehension and vocabulary
- build first two-path evidence

### Flow

1. Daily Encounter: important word from a short text.
2. Read a short text together.
3. Wordcraft: identify two important or unknown words.
4. Insight: write one gist statement.
5. Revise the gist after discussion.
6. Character Log: Insight or Wordcraft evidence entry.

### Evidence

- word notes
- gist statement
- revised gist

## Day 5: First Mini Quest

### Goals

- complete a low-stakes quest
- practise the weekly structure
- reflect on first growth evidence

### Flow

1. Launch mini quest with a short text.
2. Voice: reread selected lines.
3. Wordcraft: unlock one key word.
4. Insight: answer one evidence question.
5. Submit solo evidence.
6. Complete weekly reflection.

### Evidence

- one solo answer
- one word strategy
- one reflection

## Week 1 XP guidance

Keep XP simple.

Suggested awards:

- 10 XP for setting up the passport
- 10 XP for completing a training task
- 15 XP for using a named strategy
- 20 XP for submitting first evidence
- 10 XP for completing reflection

Do not make XP the main focus of Week 1.

## Materials needed

- student overview
- Guild Passport
- short shared text
- exit tickets
- Voice / Insight / Wordcraft display
- teacher tracker

## Teacher check at end of week

Ask:

- Do students understand the three paths?
- Did the game layer feel mature enough?
- Did any part create comparison or shame?
- Can students complete a Character Log in under five minutes?
- Which routine needs reteaching next week?

## Adjustment rule

If students are confused, slow down the game mechanics. Protect the reading routine first.
