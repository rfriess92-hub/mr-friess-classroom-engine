# Literacy Guild Campaign Map

## Purpose

This file defines the controlled-choice campaign model for the Literacy Guild.

Students should have autonomy in how they move through the campaign, but the teacher still protects required skill coverage.

## Core design rule

Students choose the route. The teacher controls the map.

Every student eventually completes the required quests for the arc, but students may have some choice over:

- quest order
- text option
- evidence format
- support level
- party role
- side quests
- personal goal focus

Students may not use choice to avoid an entire path or skill.

## Quest types

| Quest type | Meaning | Required? |
|---|---|---|
| Core Quest | Teaches or practises an essential skill for the arc | Yes |
| Choice Quest | Students choose from options that assess the same target | Usually one required from a set |
| Side Quest | Optional extra practice, XP, title, or ability evidence | No |
| Recovery Quest | Targeted reteaching or extra practice | Required only when needed |
| Boss Quest | Larger evidence task at the end of an arc | Yes |
| Portfolio Quest | Reflection and evidence review | Yes at major checkpoints |

## Campaign movement rules

1. Each arc has required destinations.
2. Students may choose the order of some quests.
3. Some quests are locked until earlier evidence is complete.
4. Boss quests require core quest completion.
5. Side quests never replace required evidence.
6. Recovery quests are framed as training routes, not punishment.
7. The Game Master can reroute students when evidence shows a need.

## Completion rule

A quest is complete when the student has:

- attempted the target skill
- used or practised the named strategy
- submitted evidence
- reflected or logged the result
- received teacher check when required

Completion does not mean perfect performance.

## Arc structure

Each arc should include:

- arc title
- main path
- support paths
- required core quests
- choice quests
- optional side quests
- one boss quest
- progress check connection
- portfolio or reflection point

## Arc 1: Enter the Guild

### Purpose

Introduce the course, dignity rules, three paths, character identity, party roles, and evidence routines.

### Main path

All paths equally.

### Required quests

| Quest | Focus | Completion evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Enter the Guild | Course identity and norms | Guild Passport started |
| Learn the Loop | Train, attempt, feedback, revise, log | Revised small task |
| Path Finder | Voice / Insight / Wordcraft difference | Path sort or reflection |
| First Evidence | Low-stakes solo evidence | One submitted evidence item |

### Choice options

Students may choose:

- first personal training goal
- starting class
- crest or symbol
- first path interest

### Boss quest

First Mini Quest: complete one short text task using at least two paths.

### Progress check connection

Private baseline checks and teacher observations inform first goals.

## Arc 2: Three-Path Training

### Purpose

Teach the basic routines for Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft before narrowing into a path-specific arc.

### Main path

All paths equally.

### Required quests

| Quest | Focus | Completion evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Echo Cast | Voice | Reread note or fluency reflection |
| Gist Map | Insight | First and revised gist |
| Context Torch | Wordcraft | Context clue explanation |
| Three-Path Mini Quest | Integration | Solo evidence using at least two paths |

### Choice options

Students may choose:

- order of Echo Cast, Gist Map, and Context Torch when schedule allows
- text option from teacher-approved set
- solo evidence format from approved options
- first party role

### Boss quest

Three-Path Mini Quest.

### Progress check connection

Teacher notes which students understand the paths and which need reteaching.

## Arc 3: Voice Trial

### Purpose

Build safe fluency routines through rereading, phrasing, punctuation, and self-correction.

### Main path

Voice.

### Support paths

Insight and Wordcraft support comprehension and difficult words inside fluency texts.

### Required quests

| Quest | Focus | Completion evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Echo Reading Quest | Repeat a model read | Reread reflection |
| Second Read Quest | Improve through rereading | First/second read comparison note |
| Phrase Builder Quest | Chunk sentences and use punctuation | Marked text or recording reflection |
| Error Recovery Quest | Notice and fix miscues | Self-correction note |
| Voice Boss Quest | Demonstrate fluency strategy growth | Recording, conference, or reflection |

### Choice options

Students may choose the order of the first three Voice quests.

Error Recovery unlocks after at least two earlier Voice quests.

Voice Boss unlocks after all required Voice quests are complete.

### Side quests

- Partner Rehearsal
- Performance Without Pressure
- Punctuation Shield Practice
- Confident Voice Reflection

### Progress check connection

Use ORF, recordings, teacher observations, and student reflection privately.

## Arc 4: Word Vault

### Purpose

Build vocabulary power through context clues, word parts, word maps, and precise usage.

### Main path

Wordcraft.

### Support paths

Insight supports meaning. Voice supports oral use and pronunciation when appropriate.

### Required quests

| Quest | Focus | Completion evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Context Clue Quest | Infer meaning from nearby words | Context clue explanation |
| Prefix / Suffix Quest | Use common affixes | Word breakdown |
| Root / Base Word Quest | Break word into meaningful parts | Word part explanation |
| Word Use Quest | Use target word correctly | Sentence or oral explanation |
| Wordcraft Boss Quest | Demonstrate vocabulary strategy use | Word map plus usage task |

### Choice options

Students may choose the order of Context, Affix, and Root/Base quests.

Word Use unlocks after at least two word strategy quests.

Wordcraft Boss unlocks after all required Wordcraft quests are complete.

### Side quests

- Word Collector
- Precision Upgrade
- Domain Key
- Word Mentor

### Progress check connection

Use teacher-created vocabulary evidence. DIBELS does not directly supply a Wordcraft score.

## Arc 5: Insight Gate

### Purpose

Build comprehension through gist, evidence, inference, questioning, and repair strategies.

### Main path

Insight.

### Support paths

Wordcraft supports key vocabulary. Voice supports rereading for meaning.

### Required quests

| Quest | Focus | Completion evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Gist Quest | Say what a section is mostly about | Gist statement |
| Evidence Quest | Support answer with text | Answer with detail or quote |
| Inference Quest | Explain what text suggests | Inference plus clue |
| Repair Route Quest | Use a strategy when confused | Repair strategy reflection |
| Insight Boss Quest | Demonstrate comprehension strategy use | Evidence-based response |

### Choice options

Students may choose order of Gist and Evidence if both are taught.

Inference unlocks after Gist and Evidence.

Repair Route can happen whenever confusion appears, but it is required before the boss quest.

### Side quests

- Question Builder
- Evidence Hunter
- Discussion Move
- Summary Upgrade

### Progress check connection

Use Maze only as one comprehension signal. Classroom evidence remains essential.

## Arc 6: Three-Path Campaign

### Purpose

Students complete integrated quests using Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft together.

### Main path

Rotates by quest.

### Required quests

| Quest | Focus | Completion evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Archive Quest | Read, unlock words, explain gist | Integrated response |
| Evidence Trail | Build answer from text details | Evidence response |
| Word and Meaning Quest | Use vocabulary to deepen comprehension | Wordcraft plus Insight evidence |
| Reread to Understand | Use Voice to support comprehension | Reread reflection and answer revision |
| Three-Path Boss Quest | Demonstrate integrated growth | Portfolio-ready evidence |

### Choice options

Students may choose from teacher-approved quests, but must complete at least one quest where each path is the main path.

### Side quests

- Party Strategy Challenge
- Independent Evidence Challenge
- Revision Route
- Mentor Move

### Progress check connection

Use evidence logs, conferences, and progress checks to identify remaining gaps.

## Arc 7: Final Guild Portfolio

### Purpose

Students review evidence, explain growth, and plan next steps.

### Required quests

| Quest | Focus | Completion evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Evidence Sort | Choose strongest evidence | Portfolio selections |
| Growth Reflection | Explain what improved | Written or oral reflection |
| Path Review | Review Voice, Insight, Wordcraft | Three-path reflection |
| Final Conference | Discuss progress and next goal | Conference note |
| Final Portfolio Quest | Submit final growth snapshot | Portfolio package |

### Choice options

Students choose which evidence pieces to feature, but must include at least one item connected to each path.

## Tracking required completion

The teacher tracker should mark:

- required quests completed
- side quests completed
- boss quest readiness
- missing path evidence
- conference needs
- recovery quest needs

## Student-facing map rule

You can choose your route, but every Guild member eventually visits the required places on the map.

The goal is not to rush. The goal is to collect evidence that shows your reading strategies are getting stronger.
