# Individual Character System

## Purpose

This file defines the individual student character layer for the Literacy Guild.

The character system should make growth visible without turning reading ability into a public identity.

## Core rule

A character is not a reading level.

A character represents:

- training habits
- strategy use
- evidence collected
- path growth
- reflection
- persistence
- contribution to quests

A character does not represent:

- intelligence
- reading level
- DIBELS score
- ability group
- speed compared to classmates
- public rank against peers

## Character creation

Each student creates a character during launch.

Recommended fields:

- character name
- guild title
- crest or symbol
- starting class
- path interest
- personal training goal
- preferred party role

Keep character creation simple. The goal is student ownership, not a long art project.

## Starting classes

Classes are playstyles. They are not ability groups.

Students may choose any class.

| Class | Student-facing identity | Strong classroom use |
|---|---|---|
| Pathfinder | I help find the way through a text. | gist, structure, main idea |
| Wordsmith | I unlock important words. | vocabulary, morphology, word choice |
| Scout | I preview, notice, and ask useful questions. | text features, predictions, questions |
| Bard | I work with voice, phrasing, and discussion. | fluency, oral reading, talk |
| Scribe | I build and improve written evidence. | written responses, revision |
| Strategist | I choose tools and next moves. | goal-setting, reflection, repair strategies |
| Lorekeeper | I connect ideas and meaning. | themes, background, connections |

## Class selection rules

- Students choose their class.
- The teacher may guide but should not assign classes based on weakness.
- Classes can change at checkpoints.
- No class is stronger than another.
- A class gives identity, not an academic label.

## Individual level

Individual level shows campaign progress.

Suggested levels:

| Level | Title | Meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Initiate | I have entered the Guild and started training. |
| 2 | Apprentice | I have completed early training and logged evidence. |
| 3 | Explorer | I am attempting quests with more independence. |
| 4 | Specialist | I have confirmed growth in at least one path. |
| 5 | Guild Member | I have completed a boss task and can show evidence. |
| 6 | Adept | I have evidence across Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft. |

## Path ranks

Path ranks are separate from level.

- Voice rank shows fluency evidence.
- Insight rank shows comprehension evidence.
- Wordcraft rank shows vocabulary evidence.

A student can be Level 4 and still be Rank 1 in one path. That is acceptable. Level shows campaign progress. Rank shows specific evidence.

## Individual abilities

Abilities are named strategies. They do not bypass learning.

### Voice abilities

| Ability | Student meaning |
|---|---|
| Echo Cast | I can repeat a model read. |
| Second Read Surge | I improve on a reread. |
| Punctuation Shield | I use punctuation to guide phrasing. |
| Error Recovery | I notice and fix a reading error. |
| Confident Voice | I explain a fluency strategy. |

### Insight abilities

| Ability | Student meaning |
|---|---|
| Gist Map | I can say what a section is mostly about. |
| Evidence Anchor | I support my answer with the text. |
| Inference Leap | I explain what the text suggests. |
| Confusion Signal | I notice when meaning breaks. |
| Repair Route | I choose a strategy to rebuild meaning. |

### Wordcraft abilities

| Ability | Student meaning |
|---|---|
| Context Torch | I use nearby words to infer meaning. |
| Root Strike | I break a word into meaningful parts. |
| Word Web | I connect a word to examples and related words. |
| Precision Upgrade | I replace vague words with stronger words. |
| Domain Key | I use academic words correctly. |

## Unlocking abilities

Students unlock an ability by showing evidence of the strategy.

Ability unlocks should require:

1. the strategy was taught
2. the student used it
3. the student logged evidence
4. the teacher confirmed it

Abilities can be displayed privately in the Guild Passport or on a personal character sheet.

## Individual rewards

Safe individual rewards:

- title unlocks
- ability unlocks
- passport stamps
- rank-up notes
- private teacher feedback
- choice of next quest focus
- chance to mentor a strategy briefly

Avoid rewards that publicly expose who is far ahead or far behind.

## Student-facing explanation

Your character grows when you train, use strategies, collect evidence, and reflect on your next move. Your character is not your reading level. It shows what you are learning to do.

## Teacher caution

Keep the character system light. If students spend more time decorating characters than training reading skills, simplify it.
