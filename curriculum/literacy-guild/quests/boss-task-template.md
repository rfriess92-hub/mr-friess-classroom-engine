# Boss Task Template

## Purpose

A boss task is a larger demonstration after training. It should feel important, but it is not a surprise test.

A boss task should show what each student can do with the skill after practice, feedback, and support.

## Student-facing setup

Write a short setup that explains the challenge.

Example:

You have trained with this text all week. Now your job is to show your best evidence of growth in Voice, Insight, or Wordcraft.

## Skill target

Students will show they can:

- use the target strategy
- produce evidence
- explain what improved or what they now understand

## Required evidence

Choose one main evidence product.

Options:

- fluency recording and reflection
- evidence-based answer
- short paragraph
- word strategy explanation
- revised response
- oral conference
- combined three-path task

## Success criteria

A successful boss task includes:

- clear attempt at the target skill
- evidence from the text or task
- use of a taught strategy
- reflection on the next move

## Supports allowed

Possible supports:

- rereading
- anchor chart
- strategy card
- word bank
- teacher prompt
- partner rehearsal
- audio support
- sentence frame

Supports should help students show learning. They should not hide whether the student understands the skill.

## Boss task format

### Step 1: Prepare

Review the target skill and success criteria.

### Step 2: Attempt

Complete the main task independently or with allowed supports.

### Step 3: Improve

Use feedback, rereading, or revision to improve the work.

### Step 4: Submit evidence

Submit the final evidence product.

### Step 5: Reflect

Answer one short reflection prompt.

## Reflection prompts

Choose one:

- What strategy did you use?
- What changed from your first attempt to your final attempt?
- What evidence shows your growth?
- What should you train next?

## XP and rank connection

Possible XP:

- Evidence XP
- Feedback XP
- Reflection XP
- Growth XP

Possible rank evidence:

- Voice rank evidence
- Insight rank evidence
- Wordcraft rank evidence

Teacher confirmation required for rank-up.

## Teacher scoring notes

Use a simple three-point check:

| Check | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Not yet | Needs more training or clearer evidence |
| Evidence shown | Skill is visible in this task |
| Strong evidence | Skill is clear and can support rank progress |

## Teacher caution

Do not make every Friday a high-pressure boss task. Some Fridays can be level-up reflection, conference, portfolio work, or catch-up evidence.
