# Insight Gate Arc Packet

## Purpose

Insight Gate is the comprehension path arc.

It builds students' ability to understand text, explain gist, use evidence, make inferences, notice confusion, and repair meaning when reading breaks down.

Insight means students are learning how to understand, explain, and prove their thinking about a text.

## Placement

Recommended placement:

- Full-year course: February-March
- Semester course: Weeks 8-9

Begin this arc after students understand the three paths, Guild Passport, basic ability cards, solo evidence, and controlled quest choice.

## Arc length

- Full-year: 4-6 weeks
- Semester: about 2 weeks

For the semester version, compress the sequence and keep comprehension evidence short and clear.

## Main path

Insight = Comprehension

## Support paths

Voice supports rereading for meaning.

Wordcraft supports key vocabulary that affects understanding.

| Ability | Student meaning |

| Confusion Signal | I can notice when meaning breaks down. |
| Repair Route | I can use a strategy to rebuild meaning. |

## Required quests

| Quest | Ability | Required evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Gist Quest | Gist Map | gist statement and revision |
| Evidence Quest | Evidence Anchor | answer with text detail |
| Inference Quest | Inference Leap | inference plus clue |
| Repair Route Quest | Confusion Signal / Repair Route | confusion note and repair strategy |
| Insight Boss Quest | Integrated Insight use | evidence-based response and reflection |

## Choice and unlock rules

Students may choose the order of Gist Quest and Evidence Quest when scheduling allows.

Inference Quest unlocks after students have practised gist and evidence.

Repair Route Quest can happen whenever confusion appears, but it is required before the boss task.

Insight Boss unlocks after required Insight core quests are complete.

Choice cannot be used to avoid solo comprehension evidence.

## Text selection

Use short texts that reward thinking but are not so long that students lose the strategy.

Good options:

- short narrative passages
- high-interest nonfiction paragraphs
- paired short texts
- images with captions
- short articles
- teacher-created scenarios
- notices, letters, or short speeches

Avoid:

- long unsupported texts
- questions that can be answered without reading
- trick questions
- evidence tasks with too many steps at once
- comprehension work that becomes only worksheet completion

## Quest 1: Gist Quest

Target: I can say what a section is mostly about.

Training:

1. Teacher reads or displays a short text section.
2. Students identify who or what the section is about.
3. Students identify what is most important about it.
4. Students write a first gist statement.
5. Students revise after rereading or discussion.

Solo evidence:

- My first gist was:
- I revised it to:
- I changed it because:

Completion: student writes a gist statement and revises it for clarity or accuracy.

## Quest 2: Evidence Quest

Target: I can support my thinking with a detail from the text.

Training:

1. Teacher asks a focused question.
2. Students answer in simple language.
3. Students find one useful text detail.
4. Students connect the detail to the answer.

Solo evidence:

- My answer is:
- The text detail that supports this is:
- This detail helps because:

Completion: student answers and uses one relevant text detail.

## Quest 3: Inference Quest

Target: I can explain what the text suggests.

Training:

1. Teacher models the difference between stated and suggested information.
2. Students identify a clue in the text.
3. Students make an inference.
4. Students explain how the clue supports the inference.

Solo evidence:

- I infer that:
- A clue from the text is:
- This clue suggests that because:

Completion: student makes a reasonable inference and explains the clue.

## Quest 4: Repair Route Quest

Target: I can notice confusion and use a repair strategy.

Training:

1. Teacher models a place where meaning breaks down.
2. Teacher names the confusion.
3. Teacher models one repair move.
4. Students mark a confusing part or possible breakdown.
5. Students choose a repair strategy.

Repair options:

- reread the sentence
- reread the paragraph
- check a key word
- ask a question
- summarize what is known so far
- look for evidence
- slow down and chunk the text

Solo evidence:

- I got confused when:
- The repair strategy I used was:
- After using it, I understood:

Completion: student identifies confusion and names a repair strategy.

## Side Quest: Question Builder

Purpose: practise asking useful questions about a text.

Evidence:

- one question about the text
- answer or possible answer
- explanation of why the question matters

## Side Quest: Evidence Hunter

Purpose: strengthen the habit of finding useful details.

Evidence:

- answer
- two possible details
- choice of strongest detail
- explanation of why it is strongest

## Side Quest: Summary Upgrade

Purpose: improve a weak summary or gist statement.

Evidence:

- first summary or gist
- revised version
- note explaining what improved

## Recovery Quest: Comprehension Reroute

Use when a student needs more support before boss evidence.

Support options:

- shorter text chunk
- read-aloud or audio support
- teacher table questioning
- sentence frames
- highlighted evidence choices
- oral rehearsal before writing
- paired discussion before solo evidence

Evidence:

- one short answer
- one supporting clue or detail
- one next-step note

## Insight Boss Quest

Purpose: demonstrate comprehension strategy growth.

Students choose one approved evidence format.

| Option | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Written response | answer with gist, evidence, and explanation |
| Conference | teacher conference explaining gist, evidence, and inference |
| Annotated text | marked text plus written reflection |
| Revision package | first answer, feedback, revised answer, reflection |

Required components:

1. selected text or text section
2. named Insight strategy
3. gist or main idea
4. evidence from the text
5. explanation or inference
6. next Insight goal

Student reflection:

- The Insight strategy I used was:
- The text is mostly about:
- Evidence that helped me was:
- My thinking changed or grew because:
- My next Insight goal is:

Completion: student demonstrates a comprehension strategy and explains how it helped.

## XP guidance

| Action | XP |
|---|---:|
| Completed useful Insight practice | 10 |
| Used a named Insight strategy | 15 |
| Submitted clear comprehension evidence | 20 |
| Revised after feedback | 15 |
| Reflected on next Insight goal | 10 |
| Used evidence effectively | 15 |

Award in batches. Do not turn comprehension into point accounting.

## Progress check connection

Maze or other comprehension checks may help with planning, but classroom evidence is still needed.

Use classroom evidence, conferences, revised responses, and repair notes to describe growth.

Progress checks guide instruction. They do not become public game status.

## Teacher tracker focus

Track only:

- current Insight quest
- comprehension evidence submitted
- strategy used
- reroute need
- boss readiness
- next teacher-table move

## Student-facing arc script

Insight Gate is about understanding what you read and proving your thinking. You are learning how to find the gist, use evidence, make inferences, and repair meaning when a text gets confusing.

## End-of-arc teacher review

Ask:

1. Who can write or say a clear gist?
2. Who can support an answer with evidence?
3. Who can explain a reasonable inference?
4. Who notices confusion and uses a repair move?
5. Who needs a Comprehension Reroute?
6. Who has enough evidence for an Insight rank-up?

## Report-card comment evidence

Useful evidence from this arc can support comments about:

- identifying gist
- using text evidence
- making inferences
- repairing comprehension
- revising answers after feedback
- growing confidence when explaining thinking
- needing continued support with comprehension strategies

## Design caution

Insight work must stay focused on meaning.

Do not let comprehension tasks become only copying, guessing, or worksheet completion.

The goal is clearer understanding and stronger evidence of thinking.
