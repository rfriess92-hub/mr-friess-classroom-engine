# Final Guild Portfolio Packet

## Purpose

The Final Guild Portfolio is the capstone for the literacy intervention course.

Students select evidence, explain reading growth, identify useful strategies, and set next reading goals.

This is a reading-growth portfolio for reflection, conferences, and report-card comment evidence. It is not a graded ELA portfolio.

## Placement

Recommended placement:

- Full-year course: June
- Semester course: final 1-2 weeks

Use this after students have enough Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft evidence to reflect meaningfully.

## Main path

All paths:

- Voice = Fluency
- Insight = Comprehension
- Wordcraft = Vocabulary

## Required portfolio quests

| Quest | Purpose | Required evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Evidence Sort | Choose useful evidence | selected evidence from each path |
| Growth Reflection | Explain growth | written or oral reflection |
| Path Review | Review Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft | one note for each path |
| Final Conference | Discuss progress privately | conference note and next goal |
| Final Portfolio Quest | Submit final growth snapshot | portfolio package and reflection |

## What counts as evidence

Evidence can include:

- reread reflections
- marked phrase chunks
- gist statements
- evidence-based answers
- inference notes
- repair strategy reflections
- context clue explanations
- word maps
- revised responses
- teacher conference notes
- Character Log entries
- boss task evidence

Do not require every student to have the same evidence format.

## What not to include

Do not include public rankings, XP totals as academic evidence, party status, character level as reading level, raw screening scores, or student comparison language.

## Quest 1: Evidence Sort

Target: I can choose evidence that shows my reading growth.

Student task:

1. Look through Guild Passport, Character Log, quest evidence, and returned work.
2. Choose at least one piece of Voice evidence.
3. Choose at least one piece of Insight evidence.
4. Choose at least one piece of Wordcraft evidence.
5. Label each piece with the strategy it shows.

Student frame:

- My Voice evidence is:
- It shows:
- My Insight evidence is:
- It shows:
- My Wordcraft evidence is:
- It shows:

Completion: student selects and labels evidence for all three paths.

## Quest 2: Growth Reflection

Target: I can explain how I have grown as a reader.

Student chooses one growth focus:

- rereading more effectively
- understanding texts more clearly
- using evidence better
- unlocking words more independently
- asking for support appropriately
- attempting reading tasks with more confidence

Student frame:

- At the start, I noticed:
- Now I can:
- Evidence that shows this is:
- A strategy that helped me was:
- I still need to practise:

Completion: student explains one area of growth using evidence.

## Quest 3: Path Review

Target: I can review my growth in Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft.

Voice frame:

- A Voice strategy I used was:
- It helped me because:
- My next Voice goal is:

Insight frame:

- An Insight strategy I used was:
- It helped me because:
- My next Insight goal is:

Wordcraft frame:

- A Wordcraft strategy I used was:
- It helped me because:
- My next Wordcraft goal is:

Completion: student reflects on all three paths.

## Quest 4: Final Conference

Target: I can talk privately about my reading growth and next steps.

Conference structure:

1. Student shares one selected evidence piece.
2. Student names one strategy that helped.
3. Teacher asks one follow-up question.
4. Student chooses or confirms next reading goal.
5. Teacher records a short conference note.

Teacher questions:

- Which evidence shows your strongest growth?
- Which strategy helped you most?
- What is still hard when you read?
- What kind of support helps you most?
- What should your next reading goal be?

Completion: student participates in a private conference and leaves with a next goal.

## Quest 5: Final Portfolio Quest

Target: I can submit a final reading-growth snapshot.

Portfolio package includes:

1. one Voice evidence piece
2. one Insight evidence piece
3. one Wordcraft evidence piece
4. growth reflection
5. path review
6. next reading goal
7. teacher conference note or check

Student final reflection:

- The evidence I am most proud of is:
- It shows growth because:
- The strategy I will keep using is:
- My next reading goal is:

Completion: student submits the final portfolio package.

## Teacher use

Use the portfolio to prepare:

- report-card comments
- learning update comments
- conference notes
- next-step reading goals
- future reading group plans

Report the reading growth, not the game progress.

## Comment-writing frame

Use this structure:

1. Current strength
2. Strategy or evidence observed
3. Growth or support need
4. Next step

Frame:

`[Student] is developing stronger reading skills by ____. They are beginning to use ____ to support ____. Continued practice with ____ will help them ____.`

## Comment evidence areas

Voice evidence can support comments about rereading, phrasing, accuracy, expression, and confidence.

Insight evidence can support comments about gist, evidence use, inference, comprehension repair, and explaining thinking.

Wordcraft evidence can support comments about context clues, word parts, academic vocabulary, and precise word use.

Learning-habit evidence can support comments about feedback, independence, confidence, stamina, and support needs.

## XP guidance

Keep XP light during the portfolio.

| Action | XP |
|---|---:|
| Selected evidence for all three paths | 20 |
| Completed growth reflection | 20 |
| Completed path review | 20 |
| Participated in final conference | 20 |
| Set a clear next reading goal | 10 |

Do not let XP become the point of the portfolio.

## Teacher workflow

Recommended order:

1. Evidence Sort
2. Teacher checks for missing path evidence
3. Growth Reflection
4. Path Review
5. Final Conference
6. Final Portfolio Quest
7. Teacher drafts comments or learning-update notes

## Missing evidence plan

If a student is missing one path:

- assign one short recovery task
- use a teacher conference as evidence if appropriate
- accept oral evidence where appropriate
- focus on what the student can show now

Do not delay the whole portfolio because one evidence type is missing.

## Design caution

The portfolio should feel like a growth review, not a final exam.

The purpose is to help students see themselves as readers with strategies, evidence, and next steps.
