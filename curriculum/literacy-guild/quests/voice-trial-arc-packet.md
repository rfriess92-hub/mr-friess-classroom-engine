# Voice Trial Arc Packet

## Purpose

Voice Trial is the first path-specific arc after launch and Three-Path Training.

Voice means fluency: students practise reading more smoothly, accurately, and confidently through modelling, rereading, phrasing, punctuation, and self-correction.

## Placement

Recommended placement:

- Full-year course: October-November
- Semester course: Weeks 4-5

Begin this arc after students understand the three paths, Guild Passport, basic ability cards, and solo evidence.

## Arc length

- Full-year: 4-6 weeks
- Semester: about 2 weeks

For the semester version, compress the sequence and reduce side quests.

## Main path

Voice = Fluency

## Support paths

Insight helps students notice whether rereading improves meaning.

Wordcraft helps students handle words that interrupt fluency.

| Ability | Student meaning |

| Quest | Ability | Required evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Echo Reading Quest | Echo Cast | Reread reflection |
| Second Read Quest | Second Read Surge | First/second read comparison |
| Phrase Builder Quest | Punctuation Shield | Marked text or phrasing reflection |
| Error Recovery Quest | Error Recovery | Self-correction note |
| Voice Boss Quest | Confident Voice | Recording, conference, or written fluency reflection |

## Choice and unlock rules

Students may choose the order of the first three Voice quests when scheduling allows.

Error Recovery unlocks after at least two earlier Voice quests.

Voice Boss unlocks after required Voice core quests are complete.

Choice cannot be used to skip rereading, reflection, or solo evidence.

## Text selection

Use short texts students can access with support.

Good options:

- short narrative passages
- high-interest nonfiction paragraphs
- dialogue excerpts
- speeches or notices
- poems with clear punctuation
- teacher-created passages

Avoid cold public reading and long unsupported passages.

## Quest 1: Echo Reading Quest

Target: I can repeat a model read and notice what gets smoother.

Training:

1. Teacher models a short passage or selected lines.
2. Students follow silently.
3. Teacher rereads one sentence with clear phrasing.
4. Students echo read the sentence.
5. Students practise once with a partner or quietly to self.

Solo evidence:

- The line I practised was:
- One part that got smoother was:
- The model helped me because:

Completion: student practises after a model and names what improved.

## Quest 2: Second Read Quest

Target: I can improve after a second read.

Training:

1. Students complete a first supported read.
2. Students mark a word, phrase, or sentence that slowed them down.
3. Students reread the same passage.
4. Students compare first read and second read.

Solo evidence:

- On my first read, I noticed:
- On my second read, I improved:
- My next reread goal is:

Completion: student compares two reads with a clear improvement note.

## Quest 3: Phrase Builder Quest

Target: I can use punctuation and phrase chunks to guide my reading.

Training:

1. Teacher models a long sentence without phrasing.
2. Teacher models the same sentence with punctuation and chunks.
3. Students mark pauses or chunks.
4. Students reread using their markings.

Solo evidence options:

- marked text with phrase chunks
- short reflection on where the student paused
- recording note explaining what changed

Completion: student uses punctuation or chunking to improve a reread.

## Quest 4: Error Recovery Quest

Target: I can notice and fix some reading errors.

Training:

1. Teacher models a misread word or meaning break.
2. Teacher models stopping, rereading, and correcting.
3. Students practise with a short passage.
4. Students mark one place where they slowed down, reread, or fixed something.

Solo evidence:

- I noticed a problem when:
- I fixed or checked it by:
- This helped because:

Completion: student identifies a reading problem and explains a repair move.

## Side Quest: Partner Rehearsal

Purpose: practise fluency with a partner before solo evidence.

Evidence:

- partner rehearsal note
- one phrase improved
- one respectful feedback sentence

Rule: partner work supports solo evidence. It does not replace it.

## Side Quest: Confident Voice Reflection

Purpose: explain which Voice strategy helped most.

Evidence:

- The Voice strategy that helped me most was:
- It helped because:
- I will use it next time when:

## Recovery Quest: Safe Reread Route

Use when a student needs more protected practice before boss evidence.

Support options:

- shorter passage
- teacher echo read
- choral read
- audio support
- sentence frame
- private reread conference

Evidence:

- one supported reread
- one sentence explaining what changed

## Voice Boss Quest

Purpose: demonstrate Voice strategy growth without public performance pressure.

Students choose one approved evidence format.

| Option | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Recording | short passage recording and reflection |
| Conference | brief teacher conference and strategy explanation |
| Marked text | marked text plus written reflection |

Required components:

1. selected passage or lines
2. named Voice strategy
3. evidence of rereading or phrasing
4. reflection on growth
5. next Voice goal

Student reflection:

- The Voice strategy I used was:
- My reading improved because:
- Evidence that shows this is:
- My next Voice goal is:

Completion: student demonstrates a Voice strategy and explains how it helped.

## XP guidance

| Action | XP |
|---|---:|
| Completed useful Voice practice | 10 |
| Used a named Voice strategy | 15 |
| Submitted clear Voice evidence | 20 |
| Revised or reread after feedback | 15 |
| Reflected on next Voice goal | 10 |
| Improved from personal baseline | 20 |

Award in batches. Do not turn fluency into point accounting.

## Progress check connection

Use ORF, recordings, and teacher observations privately to notice accuracy, phrasing, hesitation, self-correction, and growth from baseline.

Progress checks guide instruction. They do not become public game status.

## Teacher tracker focus

Track only:

- current Voice quest
- Voice evidence submitted
- strategy used
- reroute need
- boss readiness
- next teacher-table move

## Student-facing arc script

Voice Trial is about reading with more control. You are training rereading, phrasing, punctuation, and recovery strategies so your reading becomes smoother and makes more sense.

## End-of-arc teacher review

Ask:

1. Who can explain a Voice strategy?
2. Who improved with rereading?
3. Who needs a Safe Reread Route?
4. Who has enough evidence for a Voice rank-up?
5. Which supports should continue into the next arc?

## Design caution

Voice work must stay safe. Avoid surprise public reading, speed contests, or public comparisons.

The goal is controlled growth, not performance pressure.
