# Word Vault Arc Packet

## Purpose

Word Vault is the Wordcraft path arc.

It builds vocabulary strategy use through context clues, word parts, word maps, academic vocabulary, and precise word use.

Wordcraft means students are learning how to unlock, understand, and use important words.

## Placement

Recommended placement:

- Full-year course: December-January
- Semester course: Weeks 6-7

Begin this arc after students understand the three paths, Guild Passport, basic ability cards, solo evidence, and controlled quest choice.

## Arc length

- Full-year: 4-6 weeks
- Semester: about 2 weeks

For the semester version, compress the sequence and keep the word work focused.

## Main path

Wordcraft = Vocabulary

## Support paths

Insight helps students connect word meaning to gist and comprehension.

Voice helps students rehearse pronunciation and oral use when appropriate.

| Precision Upgrade | I can choose a stronger or more accurate word. |
| Domain Key | I can use topic or academic vocabulary correctly. |

| Quest | Ability | Required evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Context Clue Quest | Context Torch | context clue explanation |
| Prefix / Suffix Quest | Root Strike | word-part breakdown |
| Root / Base Word Quest | Root Strike | root or base-word explanation |
| Word Use Quest | Precision Upgrade or Domain Key | sentence or oral-use evidence |
| Wordcraft Boss Quest | Word Web / Domain Key | word map plus usage reflection |

## Choice and unlock rules

Students may choose the order of the first three Wordcraft quests when scheduling allows.

Word Use unlocks after at least two word strategy quests.

Wordcraft Boss unlocks after required Wordcraft core quests are complete.

Choice cannot be used to avoid explaining word meaning in context.

## Text and word selection

Use short texts with useful vocabulary.

Good word targets:

- words central to understanding the text
- high-use academic words
- words with helpful context clues
- words with clear prefixes or suffixes
- words students may use in speaking or writing

Avoid:

- random hard words
- dictionary-copy tasks without context
- long word lists
- vocabulary quizzes that do not connect to reading
- choosing words only because they look difficult

## Quest 1: Context Clue Quest

Target: I can use nearby words to infer meaning.

Training:

1. Teacher chooses one word from a short text.
2. Teacher models reading before and after the word.
3. Students underline nearby clue words.
4. Students make a smart guess.
5. Students check whether the guess fits the sentence.

Solo evidence:

- My word was:
- My smart guess was:
- Nearby words that helped were:
- My guess fits because:

Completion: student explains word meaning using nearby clues.

## Quest 2: Prefix / Suffix Quest

Target: I can use prefixes or suffixes to help unlock meaning.

Training:

1. Teacher selects a word with a useful prefix or suffix.
2. Students identify the base word if possible.
3. Students identify the prefix or suffix.
4. Students explain how the word part changes or supports meaning.

Solo evidence:

- My word was:
- The word part I noticed was:
- I think this part means:
- This helps me understand the word because:

Completion: student explains how a prefix or suffix supports meaning.

## Quest 3: Root / Base Word Quest

Target: I can break a word into meaningful parts.

Training:

1. Teacher models breaking one word into parts.
2. Students try with a second word.
3. Students connect the word part to meaning in the text.
4. Students compare with related words when useful.

Solo evidence:

- My word was:
- A part I recognized was:
- A related word is:
- This helps me understand the text because:

Completion: student explains a root, base word, or related word connection.

## Quest 4: Word Use Quest

Target: I can use a target word accurately.

Training:

1. Students choose one target word from the approved list.
2. Students say what the word means in the text.
3. Students create an original sentence or oral explanation.
4. Students revise if the word use is unclear.

Solo evidence options:

- original sentence using the word correctly
- oral explanation checked by teacher
- revised sentence showing stronger word use

Completion: student uses the word accurately and can explain the use.

## Side Quest: Word Web

Purpose: connect one important word to examples, non-examples, related words, and use.

Evidence:

- target word
- student-friendly meaning
- example
- non-example
- related word
- sentence or explanation

## Side Quest: Domain Key

Purpose: practise topic or academic vocabulary.

Evidence:

- academic word
- meaning in this text or topic
- sentence using the word accurately
- explanation of why the word matters

## Recovery Quest: Context Reroute

Use when a student needs more support with word meaning.

Support options:

- shorter sentence
- teacher table modelling
- two-choice meaning options
- highlighted clue words
- oral rehearsal before writing
- word bank
- picture or example where appropriate

Evidence:

- one target word
- one clue
- one meaning guess
- one explanation

## Wordcraft Boss Quest

Purpose: demonstrate vocabulary strategy growth.

Students choose one approved evidence format.

| Option | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Word Map | completed word map plus reflection |
| Word Breakdown | word-part explanation plus sentence use |
| Context Explanation | context clue explanation plus revised use |
| Conference | teacher conference explaining one target word and strategy |

Required components:

1. selected word
2. named Wordcraft strategy
3. explanation of meaning in context
4. accurate use or example
5. next Wordcraft goal

Student reflection:

- The Wordcraft strategy I used was:
- My word means:
- The clue or word part that helped me was:
- I used the word correctly by:
- My next Wordcraft goal is:

Completion: student demonstrates a vocabulary strategy and explains how it helped.

## Progress check connection

Wordcraft growth usually needs teacher-created evidence.

DIBELS may help with general planning, but it does not directly show vocabulary strategy use for this arc.

Use classroom evidence, conferences, word explanations, and sentence use to assess growth.

## Teacher tracker focus

Track only:

- current Wordcraft quest
- vocabulary evidence submitted
- strategy used
- reroute need
- boss readiness
- useful words for future review

## Student-facing arc script

Word Vault is about unlocking words. You are learning how to use clues, word parts, examples, and careful word use so that unfamiliar words do not stop your reading.

## End-of-arc teacher review

Ask:

1. Who can explain a word using context?
2. Who can use a word part to support meaning?
3. Who can use a target word accurately?
4. Who needs a Context Reroute?
5. Who has enough evidence for a Wordcraft rank-up?
6. Which vocabulary supports should continue into the next arc?

## Report-card comment evidence

Useful evidence from this arc can support comments about:

- using context clues
- breaking down unfamiliar words
- using academic vocabulary
- revising word use
- growing confidence with difficult words
- needing continued support with vocabulary strategies

## Design caution

Wordcraft is not memorizing long lists.

The goal is vocabulary strategy use in context.
