# Integrated Campaign Arc Packet

## Purpose

The Integrated Campaign is the arc where students use Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft together.

The goal is to help students choose and combine reading strategies when a text requires more than one kind of thinking.

This arc prepares students for portfolio reflection and report-card comment evidence.

## Placement

Recommended placement:

- Full-year course: April-May
- Semester course: Weeks 10-13

Begin this arc after students have completed launch, Three-Path Training, and at least one path-specific arc.

## Arc length

- Full-year: 4-6 weeks
- Semester: 3-4 weeks

For the semester version, use shorter texts and fewer side quests.

## Main design rule

Each quest has one main path and two support paths.

Do not try to assess everything at once.

Example:

- Main path: Insight
- Support path: Wordcraft
- Quick path: Voice

## Required quests

| Quest | Main path | Required evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Archive Quest | Voice | reread note plus meaning check |
| Evidence Trail Quest | Insight | answer with evidence and explanation |
| Word and Meaning Quest | Wordcraft | word explanation connected to meaning |
| Reread to Understand Quest | Voice / Insight | reread reflection plus revised understanding |
| Three-Path Boss Quest | Integrated | evidence from Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft |

## Choice and unlock rules

Students may choose from approved texts, roles, and evidence formats.

The teacher controls which quests are open.

Three-Path Boss unlocks after the required integrated quests are complete.

Students cannot complete the arc by doing only their strongest path.

## Text selection

Use short, high-interest texts with enough substance for discussion.

Good options:

- short articles
- narrative scenes
- school notices or letters
- speeches or excerpts
- image-and-caption sets
- paired short texts
- teacher-created mystery, problem, or scenario texts

Avoid texts or tasks where the game story matters more than the reading.

## Quest 1: Archive Quest

Main path: Voice

Support path: Insight

Target: I can reread a short text and notice how rereading improves meaning.

Solo evidence:

- The part I reread was:
- On the second read, I noticed:
- This helped me understand:

Completion: student connects rereading to improved meaning.

## Quest 2: Evidence Trail Quest

Main path: Insight

Support path: Voice or Wordcraft

Target: I can answer a question and support it with evidence.

Solo evidence:

- My answer is:
- My evidence is:
- This evidence supports my answer because:

Completion: student submits an answer with relevant evidence and explanation.

## Quest 3: Word and Meaning Quest

Main path: Wordcraft

Support path: Insight

Target: I can explain how one important word affects meaning.

Solo evidence:

- My word is:
- It means:
- I know this because:
- This word matters because:

Completion: student explains a word and connects it to meaning in the text.

## Quest 4: Reread to Understand Quest

Main path: Voice / Insight

Support path: Wordcraft

Target: I can choose a rereading or repair strategy when meaning breaks down.

Repair options:

- reread the sentence
- reread the paragraph
- check a key word
- summarize what is known
- find evidence
- ask a focused question
- slow down and chunk the text

Solo evidence:

- I got stuck when:
- I chose this strategy:
- After using it, I understood:

Completion: student names a breakdown and uses a repair strategy.

## Side Quest: Strategy Switch

Purpose: practise choosing between Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft strategies.

Evidence:

- problem noticed
- strategy chosen
- reason for the choice
- result after using it

## Side Quest: Two-Path Proof

Purpose: submit evidence from two paths in one task.

Options:

- Voice plus Insight: reread note and gist revision
- Insight plus Wordcraft: evidence answer and word explanation
- Voice plus Wordcraft: reread note and pronunciation or word-use reflection

Completion: student shows two connected strategies.

## Recovery Quest: Integrated Reroute

Use when a student is overwhelmed by too many strategy demands.

Support options:

- reduce to one main path
- shorten the text
- give two strategy choices instead of three
- teacher table conference
- oral rehearsal before writing
- sentence frames
- highlighted evidence choices

Evidence:

- one strategy used
- one piece of evidence
- one next move

## Three-Path Boss Quest

Purpose: demonstrate integrated reading strategy growth.

Students choose one approved evidence format.

| Option | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Integrated written response | short response with Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft evidence |
| Conference | teacher conference explaining all three paths |
| Portfolio page | selected text, strategy notes, and reflection |
| Revision package | first attempt, feedback, revision, three-path reflection |

Required components:

1. selected text or text section
2. one Voice evidence note
3. one Insight evidence note
4. one Wordcraft evidence note
5. explanation of which strategy helped most
6. next reading goal

Student reflection:

- The Voice strategy I used was:
- The Insight strategy I used was:
- The Wordcraft strategy I used was:
- The strategy that helped most was:
- My evidence shows growth because:
- My next reading goal is:

Completion: student demonstrates evidence from all three paths and explains strategy use.

## XP guidance

| Action | XP |
|---|---:|
| Completed useful integrated practice | 10 |
| Used a named strategy | 15 |
| Submitted clear solo evidence | 20 |
| Revised after feedback | 15 |
| Used two paths together | 15 |
| Used all three paths in boss evidence | 25 |
| Reflected on next reading goal | 10 |

Award in batches. Do not make the integrated arc a point chase.

## Rank evidence connection

Integrated campaign evidence can support any path rank, but each rank-up still needs path-specific evidence.

| Path | Possible evidence from this arc |
|---|---|
| Voice | reread reflection, phrasing note, recording note |
| Insight | gist revision, evidence answer, inference or repair note |
| Wordcraft | context explanation, word-part note, accurate word use |

## Progress check connection

Use progress checks privately and instructionally.

This arc is useful because students show whether they can choose strategies, not just complete isolated tasks.

Classroom evidence should support report-card comments about reading strategies, confidence, comprehension, vocabulary, and independence.

## Teacher tracker focus

Track only:

- current integrated quest
- main path evidence
- support path evidence
- strategy choice
- reroute need
- boss readiness
- comment-worthy evidence

## Student-facing arc script

The Integrated Campaign is where you start combining your reading strategies. Real reading often needs more than one path. Sometimes you reread. Sometimes you check a word. Sometimes you find evidence. Your job is to choose useful strategies and show evidence of what helped.

## End-of-arc teacher review

Ask:

1. Who can choose a useful reading strategy?
2. Who combines more than one path independently?
3. Who still needs single-path support?
4. Who needs an Integrated Reroute?
5. Which evidence can support report-card comments?
6. Which students are ready for portfolio reflection?

## Report-card comment evidence

Useful evidence from this arc can support comments about combining reading strategies, using rereading to improve understanding, supporting answers with evidence, using vocabulary strategies in context, repairing comprehension, and increasing independence.

## Design caution

Integrated does not mean overloaded.

If students are confused by too many strategy demands, reduce the quest to one main path and one support path.

The goal is useful strategy choice, not mechanical completion of every path every day.
