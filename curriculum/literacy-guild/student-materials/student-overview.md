# Student Overview: Welcome to the Literacy Guild

## What this class is

This is a reading training course.

The game layer gives the class structure. The real goal is to build stronger reading strategies, collect evidence of growth, and know your next move.

## The three paths

| Path | Reading skill | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Voice | Fluency | I can read more smoothly, accurately, and confidently. |
| Insight | Comprehension | I can understand, explain, and prove my thinking. |
| Wordcraft | Vocabulary | I can unlock, understand, and use important words. |

You may focus on one path at a time, but you will train all three.

## The Guild

The whole class is the Guild.

The Guild is the learning community. We work toward shared goals, but students are not ranked against one another.

## Parties

A party is a small group inside the Guild.

Parties help with discussion, rereading, word work, rehearsal, evidence checking, and preparation for solo work.

Party work helps you train. It does not replace your own evidence.

## Your character

You will create a character.

Your character shows how you train, which strategies you use, what evidence you collect, and how you grow over time.

Your character is not your reading level.

## Starting classes

A class is a playstyle, not an ability group.

| Class | What it means |
|---|---|
| Pathfinder | I help find the way through a text. |
| Wordsmith | I unlock important words. |
| Scout | I preview, notice, and ask useful questions. |
| Bard | I work with voice, phrasing, and discussion. |
| Scribe | I build and improve written evidence. |
| Strategist | I choose tools and next moves. |
| Lorekeeper | I connect ideas and meaning. |

You can change or refine your class later.

## The Game Master

The teacher is the Game Master.

The Game Master launches quests, teaches strategies, gives feedback, checks evidence, protects private progress information, approves rank-ups, and reroutes students when more support is needed.

## Quests

A quest is a reading challenge.

Most quests include a target skill, a short text or task, training practice, party support, solo evidence, and reflection.

## Quest types

| Quest type | What it means |
|---|---|
| Core Quest | A required skill quest. Everyone completes it eventually. |
| Choice Quest | You choose from options that train the same skill. |
| Side Quest | Optional extra practice, XP, title, or ability evidence. |
| Recovery Quest | Extra training when a skill needs more practice. |
| Boss Quest | A larger proof of skill after training. |
| Portfolio Quest | A reflection and evidence review quest. |

## Choice rule

You can choose your route, but you cannot skip a whole skill forever.

The Game Master controls the map. You choose some parts of the route.

## The core loop

**Train -> Attempt -> Feedback -> Revise -> Log Evidence -> Level Up**

This means you practise a skill, try a task, get feedback, improve the work, log evidence, and move forward.

## Evidence

Evidence is proof that you used a strategy or grew in a skill.

Evidence can be a written answer, revised answer, gist statement, word strategy explanation, reread reflection, recording, conference note, or Character Log entry.

Evidence does not need to be long. It needs to be clear.

## XP

XP gives credit for useful training actions.

You can earn XP for practising, using a strategy, revising after feedback, submitting evidence, reflecting, or supporting a peer without doing the work for them.

XP is not the main goal. Evidence and growth matter more.

## Ranks and levels

| Progress type | What it means |
|---|---|
| Level | Broad campaign progress. |
| Path rank | Evidence of growth in Voice, Insight, or Wordcraft. |

A level is not a reading level.

A rank-up needs evidence and teacher confirmation.

## Ability cards

Ability cards are reading strategies with game names.

| Ability | Path | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Echo Cast | Voice | I can repeat a model read. |
| Gist Map | Insight | I can say what a section is mostly about. |
| Context Torch | Wordcraft | I can use nearby words to infer meaning. |

You unlock abilities by using the strategy and showing evidence.

## Reroutes

A reroute is not failure.

A reroute means you need a different training path before moving forward.

A reroute might include teacher table, rereading, partner rehearsal, audio support, sentence frames, a smaller chunk of text, or more practice with the same strategy.

## What success looks like

You are successful when you can name the skill, use a strategy, explain your evidence, revise when needed, reflect on your next move, and show growth from your own starting point.
