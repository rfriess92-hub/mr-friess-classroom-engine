# Ability Cards

## Purpose

Ability cards name real reading strategies in game language.

They are not magic shortcuts. A student unlocks an ability by using the strategy and showing evidence.

## How to use

Print, cut, or display these as reference cards.

Each card should include:

- ability name
- path
- what it means
- when to use it
- evidence needed

## Voice ability cards

### Echo Cast

Path: Voice

Meaning: I can repeat a model read.

Use when:

- the text is new
- the sentence is hard to phrase
- I need to hear the rhythm first

Evidence:

- I practised after a model read
- I marked one part I improved

### Second Read Surge

Path: Voice

Meaning: I improve on a reread.

Use when:

- my first read is choppy
- I miss words
- I need more confidence

Evidence:

- I completed a second read
- I can explain what got smoother or more accurate

### Punctuation Shield

Path: Voice

Meaning: I use punctuation to guide phrasing.

Use when:

- sentences are long
- I am reading too fast
- I am not pausing in the right places

Evidence:

- I marked punctuation
- I used pauses or phrasing during a reread

### Error Recovery

Path: Voice

Meaning: I notice and fix a reading error.

Use when:

- a word does not sound right
- the sentence stops making sense
- I skip or replace a word

Evidence:

- I stopped, reread, and corrected myself
- I can name what I fixed

### Confident Voice

Path: Voice

Meaning: I can explain a fluency strategy.

Use when:

- I need to reflect on my reading
- I am helping someone understand a strategy

Evidence:

- I explain which strategy helped me and why

## Insight ability cards

### Gist Map

Path: Insight

Meaning: I can say what a section is mostly about.

Use when:

- I finish a chunk of text
- I feel lost
- I need to summarize before moving on

Evidence:

- I write or say a clear gist statement

### Evidence Anchor

Path: Insight

Meaning: I support my answer with the text.

Use when:

- I answer a question
- I make a claim
- I need proof

Evidence:

- I point to, quote, or paraphrase a useful detail

### Inference Leap

Path: Insight

Meaning: I explain what the text suggests.

Use when:

- the answer is not directly stated
- I need to read between the lines

Evidence:

- I explain my inference and the clue that supports it

### Confusion Signal

Path: Insight

Meaning: I notice when meaning breaks.

Use when:

- I am reading words but not understanding
- I cannot explain the section
- something does not fit

Evidence:

- I mark the confusing part and name the problem

### Repair Route

Path: Insight

Meaning: I choose a strategy to rebuild meaning.

Use when:

- I am confused
- my answer does not have evidence
- I need to reread with a plan

Evidence:

- I use rereading, questioning, summarizing, or evidence checking

## Wordcraft ability cards

### Context Torch

Path: Wordcraft

Meaning: I use nearby words to infer meaning.

Use when:

- I meet an unknown word
- I can use the sentence around it

Evidence:

- I explain which nearby clues helped me

### Root Strike

Path: Wordcraft

Meaning: I break a word into meaningful parts.

Use when:

- the word has a prefix, suffix, root, or base word
- I recognize part of the word

Evidence:

- I break the word apart and explain the parts

### Word Web

Path: Wordcraft

Meaning: I connect a word to examples and related words.

Use when:

- I need to remember a word
- I need to understand how it connects to other ideas

Evidence:

- I create a word web with examples, non-examples, or related words

### Precision Upgrade

Path: Wordcraft

Meaning: I replace vague words with stronger words.

Use when:

- my writing uses words like good, bad, thing, stuff, or nice
- I need a more exact word

Evidence:

- I revise a sentence using a stronger word

### Domain Key

Path: Wordcraft

Meaning: I use academic words correctly.

Use when:

- a subject word matters
- I need to explain an idea clearly

Evidence:

- I use the target word correctly in speech or writing

## Teacher note

Do not unlock every ability at once. Introduce cards as strategies are taught.
