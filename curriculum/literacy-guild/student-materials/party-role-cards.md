# Party Role Cards

## Purpose

Party role cards give each student a useful job during group work.

Roles are practice opportunities. They are not ability labels.

## Role rules

- Roles rotate.
- No role belongs to one student forever.
- A role does not mean the student is strongest in that skill.
- Party work supports solo evidence. It does not replace it.

## Quest Captain

Job: Keep the party focused on the task and time.

Useful phrases:

- What is our next step?
- Which part are we working on now?
- We need to get evidence before time is up.

Do:

- keep the group moving
- invite quieter members in
- check the task directions

Do not:

- boss people around
- do the work for others

## Voicekeeper

Job: Support rereading, phrasing, and oral rehearsal.

Useful phrases:

- Let's reread that sentence.
- Where should we pause?
- Which part sounded smoother the second time?

Do:

- help the group practise reading clearly
- notice punctuation
- encourage safe rehearsal

Do not:

- force someone to read publicly
- laugh at mistakes

## Insight Keeper

Job: Check meaning, gist, and understanding.

Useful phrases:

- What is this section mostly about?
- Does that answer make sense?
- What part of the text helps us know?

Do:

- ask meaning questions
- help the group summarize
- notice confusion

Do not:

- accept an answer with no evidence
- let the group move on while confused

## Wordsmith

Job: Track key words and word strategies.

Useful phrases:

- Which words matter most here?
- Can context help us?
- Can we break this word into parts?

Do:

- collect important words
- look for context clues
- notice prefixes, suffixes, roots, and base words

Do not:

- just copy dictionary definitions
- choose random words that do not matter

## Evidence Hunter

Job: Make sure answers connect to evidence.

Useful phrases:

- Where does the text show that?
- Can we point to a line or detail?
- Is that evidence strong enough?

Do:

- find text details
- check that claims have support
- help prepare solo evidence

Do not:

- give someone the whole answer
- let evidence become decoration

## Support Checker

Job: Make sure everyone has a useful way into the task.

Useful phrases:

- What job does everyone have?
- Who needs the question repeated?
- What support can we use?

Do:

- notice if someone is left out
- suggest a support or strategy
- help the party stay respectful

Do not:

- speak for everyone
- turn support into doing the work

## Party reflection

At the end of party work, answer one:

- Which role helped the group most today?
- What did our party prepare for solo evidence?
- What strategy did we use well?
- What should our party improve next time?
