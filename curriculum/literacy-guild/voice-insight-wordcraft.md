# Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft

## Voice

Teacher anchor: Fluency

Students build accuracy, phrasing, smoothness, confidence, and rereading habits.

Sample evidence:

- reread a passage with effort
- improve accuracy after practice
- use punctuation to guide phrasing
- notice and correct an error
- reflect on what improved

## Insight

Teacher anchor: Comprehension

Students build gist, evidence, inference, and meaning-monitoring skills.

Sample evidence:

- state what a section is mostly about
- support an answer with a text detail
- explain an inference
- notice confusion and try a repair strategy
- revise an answer after discussion or feedback

## Wordcraft

Teacher anchor: Vocabulary

Students build word awareness, context clue use, morphology, and academic word use.

Sample evidence:

- identify important or unknown words
- use context to infer meaning
- break a word into meaningful parts
- use a new word correctly
- teach a word strategy to a peer

## Design rule

Each path should be visible to students and useful to the teacher. The path language gives the RPG layer; the anchor language keeps the instruction clear.
