# Literacy Guild Course Architecture

## Purpose

This file defines how the Literacy Guild runs as a course.

It connects the game engine, literacy paths, character system, guild/party system, DIBELS progress model, quests, student materials, and teacher materials into one operating structure.

## Course promise

The Literacy Guild is a reading growth system.

Students train reading skills, collect evidence, earn progress, and reflect on next steps.

Students are not ranked against each other. Private progress data stays private.

## Core operating loop

The course runs on the Guild Engine loop:

**Train -> Attempt -> Feedback -> Revise -> Log Evidence -> Level Up**

Every week should include this loop in some form.

## Controlled-choice campaign model

The campaign uses controlled choice.

Students choose the route. The teacher controls the map.

This means:

- students may choose some quest order
- students may choose approved text or evidence options
- students may complete optional side quests
- students may receive recovery quests when more training is needed
- every student eventually completes required core quests
- boss quests unlock after required evidence is complete

Choice gives autonomy. It does not allow students to avoid an entire path.

## Daily structure

Default 79-minute structure:

| Time | Segment | Purpose |
|---:|---|---|
| 0-7 min | Daily Encounter | Warm-up, retrieval, word work, sentence work, or short reading move |
| 7-20 min | Guild Lesson | Explicit instruction and strategy modeling |
| 20-65 min | Training Rotations | Teacher table, independent/computer station, party/paper station |
| 65-73 min | Solo Evidence | Individual proof of skill |
| 73-79 min | Character Log | XP, evidence note, reflection, or next goal |

## Rotation model

Use three rotation types when possible:

1. Teacher Table
2. Independent or computer-supported practice
3. Party or paper-based practice

The rotation model should work even without full computer access.

## Weekly quest rhythm

Default weekly structure:

| Day | Focus | Game function | Literacy function |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | Quest Launch | Accept the quest | Introduce text, target, and key words |
| Tuesday | Skill Training | Learn or unlock a strategy | Model and practise the main path skill |
| Wednesday | Party Challenge | Train with support | Rehearse, discuss, gather evidence |
| Thursday | Evidence Build | Prepare final proof | Complete draft, recording, or response |
| Friday | Boss / Level-Up | Submit, reflect, rank-check | Demonstrate skill and set next goal |

This is the default, not a rigid rule. Short weeks can compress the rhythm.

## Path balance

Each quest should include all three paths, but only one should be the main focus.

Example:

- Main path: Insight
- Support path: Wordcraft
- Quick path: Voice

This keeps the course integrated rather than three separate mini-courses.

## Arc structure

An arc is a larger instructional focus lasting several weeks.

A good arc includes:

- repeated exposure to one main path
- recurring strategy language
- multiple evidence opportunities
- controlled quest choice
- at least one boss task
- at least one conference or progress reflection

## Recommended full-year arc map

| Time | Arc | Main purpose |
|---|---|---|
| September | Enter the Guild | Identity, routines, baselines, first evidence |
| October-November | Voice Trial | Fluency, rereading, phrasing, confidence |
| December-January | Word Vault | Vocabulary, context, morphology, academic word use |
| February-March | Insight Gate | Gist, evidence, inference, repair strategies |
| April-May | Three-Path Campaign | Integrated quests using all paths |
| June | Final Guild Portfolio | Reflection, evidence review, next-step planning |

## Recommended semester arc map

| Time | Arc | Main purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Reset and Enter the Guild | Explain new structure, launch paths, collect starting evidence |
| Weeks 2-3 | Three-Path Training | Teach routines for Voice, Insight, and Wordcraft without over-specializing |
| Weeks 4-5 | Voice Focus | Fluency growth through safe rereading and reflection |
| Weeks 6-7 | Wordcraft Focus | Vocabulary routines, word parts, context, word use |
| Weeks 8-9 | Insight Focus | Gist, evidence, inference, comprehension repair |
| Weeks 10-13 | Integrated Quests | Weekly quests using all three paths |
| Weeks 14-16 | Growth Campaign | Targeted practice and stronger independent evidence |
| Final Weeks | Portfolio and Reflection | Growth snapshot, conference, transition plan |

## Why Week 2 is not automatically Voice Trial

Week 2 should usually be Three-Path Training, not a full Voice arc yet.

Students need time to learn:

- how the Guild works
- how the daily routine works
- what the three paths mean
- how evidence is logged
- how abilities are used
- how party roles function

Starting with Voice too quickly risks narrowing the system before students understand the whole model.

## Game mechanic release schedule

Introduce mechanics slowly.

| Stage | Mechanics introduced |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | Guild, three paths, Game Master, Passport, simple XP |
| Week 2 | Character sheet, first abilities, simple party roles, student quest map preview |
| Week 3 | Rank-up requests, evidence log, first mini boss task |
| Weeks 4-5 | Party goals, ability cards, first confirmed rank-ups |
| Later | Class milestones, richer titles, portfolio layer |

Do not introduce everything at once.

## Quest choice release schedule

Introduce choice carefully.

| Stage | Student choice allowed |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | first goal, first path interest, character choices |
| Week 2 | approved evidence format and simple party role choice |
| Week 3 | small quest-order choice inside teacher-approved set |
| First path arc | choice among unlocked core quests where appropriate |
| Later arcs | side quests, recovery routes, portfolio evidence choices |

## Assessment and progress cadence

Use progress checks to guide instruction, not to drive public game status.

| Timing | Action | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Start | Baseline checks | Set private goals and teacher-table groups |
| Weekly | Solo evidence | Track path growth |
| Every 2-3 weeks | Fluency check or recording | Monitor Voice growth |
| Monthly | Maze or comprehension check | Monitor Insight growth |
| Every 6-8 weeks | Conference | Review evidence and choose next goals |
| End of arc | Boss task or portfolio check | Confirm growth and rank evidence |

## Rank-up cadence

Rank-ups should be possible but not constant.

Recommended cadence:

- students may request rank-up after evidence is logged
- teacher reviews rank-up requests weekly or biweekly
- rank-ups are confirmed privately or semi-privately
- class celebrations focus on effort and evidence, not who is highest

## Boss task cadence

Do not run a major boss task every week.

Use three levels:

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Mini boss | Every 1-2 weeks | Short demonstration of one skill |
| Arc boss | End of an arc | Larger evidence task |
| Final portfolio | End of course or year | Growth reflection and evidence review |

## Full-year vs semester differences

### Full-year Grade 8

Use:

- slower launch
- more identity building
- fuller character system
- more time for class milestones
- longer arcs
- final portfolio

Delay:

- complex rank systems
- too many ability cards
- high-pressure boss tasks

### Semester cohort

Use:

- direct reset language
- leaner mechanics
- faster routines
- clearer weekly targets
- fewer decorative elements
- more frequent conferences

Delay or reduce:

- deep lore
- large character customization
- too many titles or badges

## Minimum viable launch

By the first day, the teacher needs:

- Week 1 launch plan
- student overview
- Guild Passport
- basic character sheet
- teacher tracker
- three path display
- first short text
- first mini quest

Everything else can be introduced after routines are stable.

## What not to build yet

Do not build a full year of quest packets before the architecture is tested.

Do not build a digital platform before the paper version works.

Do not build too many badges, items, titles, or ability variants.

Do not allow quest choice to become skill avoidance.

## Success criteria

The architecture is working if:

- students can explain the three paths
- students know what evidence they need
- students understand required quests versus choice quests
- the teacher can run the tracker without losing teaching time
- character mechanics support strategy use
- parties help students prepare for solo evidence
- progress checks stay private
- the system works on a low-tech day

## Simplification rule

If the course feels too complicated, protect these five pieces:

1. daily routine
2. three literacy paths
3. solo evidence
4. private progress checks
5. reflection and next goal

Everything else can be reduced.
