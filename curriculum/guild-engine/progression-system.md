# Guild Engine Progression System

## Progression layers

The system uses three connected layers:

1. XP for training behaviours.
2. Ranks for demonstrated skill evidence.
3. Levels for overall course campaign progress.

## XP

XP should be quick to award and easy to understand. It rewards actions that lead to learning growth.

Examples:

- complete a training task
- use a target strategy
- revise after feedback
- improve from a personal baseline
- explain thinking clearly
- support a peer without doing the work for them
- complete a reflection honestly

## Ranks

Ranks are tied to specific learning paths. A student ranks up only when there is evidence.

Rank evidence should be concrete, observable, and teachable.

## Levels

Levels represent broad participation in the course campaign. A level should never mean grade level, ability group, or reading level.

## Badges and checkpoints

Badges can be used as small markers of demonstrated strategies. They should remain limited. Too many badges will create management overhead.

## Design rule

XP rewards training. Rank-ups require evidence. Levels mark campaign growth. None of these should publicly expose academic weakness.
