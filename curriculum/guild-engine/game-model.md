# Guild Engine Game Model

## Core loop

**Train -> Attempt -> Feedback -> Revise -> Log Evidence -> Level Up**

This loop is the centre of the system. Every subject implementation should preserve it.

## Student role

Students are members of a learning guild. They complete quests, practise skills, collect evidence, and rank up through demonstrated growth.

## Teacher role

The teacher is the game master and instructional coach. The teacher sets the quest, defines the evidence standard, gives feedback, protects privacy, and controls pacing.

## Game objects

- Quest: structured learning challenge.
- Training task: practice before evidence is submitted.
- Evidence: proof of skill, strategy use, revision, or growth.
- XP: credit for productive learning behaviours.
- Rank: progress inside a learning path.
- Level: broad progress through the course campaign.
- Boss task: larger demonstration of learning.
- Reflection: student explanation of what changed and what comes next.

## What the model rewards

- practice
- strategy use
- feedback use
- revision
- improvement from personal baseline
- accurate reflection
- appropriate collaboration
- evidence of skill

## What the model does not reward

- public ranking
- raw score alone
- speed alone
- strongest-student dominance
- compliance without learning evidence

## Public vs private

Public game elements can include quests, party goals, class milestones, and completed badges.

Private elements include diagnostic data, progress scores, individual conferences, and sensitive reading information.
