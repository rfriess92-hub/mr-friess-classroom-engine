# Guild Engine Design Brief

## Purpose

The Guild Engine is a reusable classroom gamification model. It is designed to make learning progress visible without turning students into public rankings.

The first classroom use case is reading intervention, but the engine should remain general enough to support other courses later.

## Design problem

Gamification can support learning when it improves persistence, feedback, goal clarity, and student agency. It becomes weak or harmful when it replaces instruction, rewards compliance only, or exposes struggling students through public comparison.

This engine should solve for:

- visible progress
- private assessment data
- repeated practice
- fast teacher feedback
- student reflection
- dignity for students working below grade level
- transferability across classes

## Core promise to students

You are not being ranked against other students. You are training specific skills, collecting evidence of growth, and learning how to improve.

## Non-negotiables

1. Character level is not academic level.
2. Private assessment data stays private.
3. XP rewards growth behaviours and training actions.
4. Rank-ups require evidence of skill.
5. Public leaderboards are not used for intervention groups.
6. The teacher controls pacing, evidence standards, and feedback.
7. The game layer must support instruction, not replace it.
8. The system must survive without full computer access.
9. Materials should be age-respectful for Grade 8-10 students.
10. The model should be reusable beyond literacy.

## Core game loop

**Train -> Attempt -> Feedback -> Revise -> Log Evidence -> Level Up**

Students should repeatedly experience:

- I practised a specific skill.
- I used a strategy.
- I received feedback.
- I improved something.
- I logged evidence.
- My character grew because I trained.

## Universal components

- Quest: a structured learning challenge.
- Training: practice before assessment.
- Evidence: proof of skill, strategy, or growth.
- XP: credit for productive learning behaviours.
- Rank: progress within a learning path.
- Level: broad campaign participation and growth.
- Boss task: a larger demonstration of learning.
- Reflection: student thinking about what changed and what comes next.
- Progress scan: private assessment information used to set goals.

## First course skin

The first course skin is the Literacy Guild.

Teacher-facing anchors:

- Fluency
- Comprehension
- Vocabulary

Student-facing paths:

- Voice
- Insight
- Wordcraft

## Deadline

The September 1, 2026 goal is classroom-ready, not complete-year-perfect.

By September 1, the system needs:

- usable game engine
- Literacy Guild implementation
- two-track implementation plan
- first three weeks of launch materials
- student materials
- teacher tracking tools
- progress check routines

It does not need a full year of quests or a polished digital platform.
