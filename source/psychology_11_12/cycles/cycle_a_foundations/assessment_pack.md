# Cycle A Foundations Assessment Pack

## Assessment Overview

Students show that they can explain psychology as a scientific field, distinguish evidence from opinion, analyze a simple claim about behaviour, and apply basic research reasoning without overclaiming.

This is a student-facing assessment source. It must not include answer keys, marking notes, or teacher-only rationale.

## Learning Targets Assessed

Students can:

- define psychology as the scientific study of behaviour and mental processes
- separate observation, inference, claim, and evidence
- explain why evidence matters in psychology
- distinguish correlation from causation in a simple example
- identify an ethical issue in a simple research scenario
- explain what information would be needed before making a stronger claim

## Student Instructions

Answer each question clearly. Use course vocabulary where it helps. You do not need to write long paragraphs unless the question asks for an explanation.

Use neutral examples and evidence from the prompt. Do not diagnose anyone. Do not make claims about people that the evidence does not support.

## Part B: Claim and Evidence

Read the claim:

> Students remember more when they study with music because music improves memory.

### 4. Claim Check

What is the claim saying?

### 5. Evidence Needed

What evidence would you need before accepting the claim?

### 6. Caution

What is one reason this claim might be too simple?

## Part C: Correlation and Causation

A survey finds that students who report sleeping more hours also report feeling more focused in class.

### 7. Correlation

What two things are related in this example?

### 8. Causation

Does this prove that more sleep causes better focus? Explain why or why not.

### 9. Third Factor

Name one other factor that could affect focus in class.

## Part D: Research Ethics

A researcher wants to study whether short practice quizzes help students remember vocabulary. The researcher plans to give one class practice quizzes and another class no practice quizzes, then compare quiz results.

### 10. Ethical Check

Name one ethical concern or fairness issue the researcher should consider.

### 11. Better Design

Suggest one way to make the study more fair or responsible.

## Part E: Scenario Application

Read the scenario:

> A teacher notices that students who complete a short review sheet before a quiz often score higher than students who do not complete it.

### 12. Careful Explanation

Write a careful psychological explanation using the words evidence, correlation, and possible cause.

### 13. Limit Statement

What can we not know from this scenario alone?

## Student Checklist

Before handing this in, check that you have:

- defined psychology
- used evidence language
- separated observation from inference
- explained correlation vs causation
- named one ethical concern
- written one limit statement
