# Cycle A Student Assessment Pack

## Foundations of Psychology: Evidence, Claims, and Limits

### Core Question

How do psychologists know what they claim to know?

### What This Pack Assesses

This assessment pack checks whether you can use psychology as a careful evidence-based discipline. You will practise defining key terms, sorting claims, separating evidence from opinion, identifying limits, checking correlation and causation, reviewing research ethics, and evaluating psychology claims in media.

This is not a personal disclosure assignment. You do not need to share private information about your mental health, family, relationships, diagnosis, therapy, or personal experiences. Use the fictional, neutral, school-based, or media-based examples provided.

### Your Job Throughout This Pack

Use careful psychology language. A strong response does not try to sound dramatic. A strong response says what the evidence supports, what it does not prove, and what a responsible person should be careful about claiming.

When you are unsure, use this sentence starter:

> Based on the evidence given, we can reasonably say ________, but we cannot conclude ________.
