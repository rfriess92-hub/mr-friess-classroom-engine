# Cycle A Foundations Capstone Packet

## Capstone Task

You will analyze a psychology claim using evidence, research reasoning, and careful limits.

Your job is not to prove a claim is true. Your job is to decide what the claim says, what evidence would be needed, and what can and cannot be concluded from the information provided.

## Learning Target

I can analyze a psychology claim by identifying evidence, avoiding overclaims, and explaining what more information would be needed.

## Big Idea

Psychology uses evidence and careful reasoning to study behaviour and mental processes.

## Task Scenario

Choose one claim to analyze.

### Claim A

Students who study with music remember more information.

### Claim B

People who practise a skill every day improve because repetition strengthens learning.

### Claim C

Students who sleep more before a test perform better because sleep improves memory.

### Claim D

People act differently in groups because social influence changes behaviour.

## Part 6: Final Explanation

Write a short explanation that answers the question:

> How should a psychology student evaluate this claim?

Your explanation should include:

- the claim
- one psychology concept
- what evidence would be needed
- one caution about correlation or causation
- one ethical issue or limitation

## Student Checklist

Before submitting, check that you have:

- chosen one claim
- identified the behaviour or mental process
- connected the claim to one psychology concept
- named evidence that would be needed
- explained a causation or correlation caution
- named one ethical issue or limitation
- avoided diagnosing anyone
- avoided claims that go beyond the evidence

## Differentiation

### Supported

Use the sentence frames and complete one claim analysis with teacher-provided vocabulary support.

Sentence frames:

- The claim says...
- The concept connected to this claim is...
- Evidence that would help is...
- This does not prove causation because...
- One ethical concern is...

### Proficient

Complete all parts for one claim and write a clear final explanation using course vocabulary.

### Extending

Compare two claims. Explain which claim would be easier to study responsibly and why.
