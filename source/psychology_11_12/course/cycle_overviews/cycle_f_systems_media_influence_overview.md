# Cycle F Overview: Systems / Media / Influence

## Core Question

How are humans shaped by designed environments, media, persuasion, and technology?

## Primary Course Function

Cycle F maps attention, behaviour design, media systems, persuasion, misinformation, social media, consumer psychology, AI, and ethical design. It should teach agency and design awareness without moral panic or student shaming.

## Module Sequence

| Module | Focus | Major Domains | Student Work | Safety / QA Control |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| F1 | Reward, novelty, and attention | Brain/body; reward; attention; habit loops; arousal. | Analyze a notification or app design. | Avoid shaming students for technology use. |
| F2 | Behaviour design | Learning; cues; rewards; friction; reinforcement. | Redesign a system to support autonomy. | Do not teach manipulation as the objective. |
| F3 | Misinformation and source confusion | Memory; cognition; fluency; cognitive load. | Media misinformation audit. | Keep politically sensitive examples neutral if needed. |
| F4 | Social comparison and identity signalling | Social psychology; identity; parasocial relationships; norms. | Analyze social media identity pressures through a fictional example. | Avoid public disclosure of online habits. |
| F5 | Consumer psychology | Scarcity; branding; status; emotion; motivation. | Advertising analysis. | Avoid body-image shaming and manipulative targeting. |
| F6 | Ethics, AI, and persuasive systems | Dark patterns; consent; autonomy; algorithms; youth audiences. | Ethical design review. | Teach agency and design awareness, not moral panic. |

## Required Through-Line Prompts

| Through-Line | Prompt |
|---|---|
| Cultural / Contextual | How do community, identity, access, representation, platform norms, and power shape the system? |
| Developmental / Lifespan | How might age, adolescence, attention, identity, and vulnerability affect media or technology influence? |
| Biological / Brain-Body | What role do reward, novelty, attention, arousal, habit loops, and stress play, and what are the limits of that explanation? |
| Ethical / Evidence | What evidence supports claims about media or technology effects, and where do persuasion, autonomy, consent, or dark patterns matter? |

## Capstone Frame

Conduct a media, technology, advertising, gaming, or consumer-system audit. Students identify psychological mechanisms, evidence, ethical concerns, cultural/contextual factors, and design recommendations.

## Student-Facing Artifact List

- Cycle opener / lens overview
- Notification/app design analysis
- Behaviour-design redesign task
- Misinformation/source-confusion audit
- Social comparison fictional scenario
- Advertising/consumer psychology analysis
- Ethical design review
- Capstone packet

## Teacher-Facing Artifact List

- Teacher binder with moral-panic and disclosure controls
- Misconception guide for media-effects, AI, attention, and persuasion claims
- Source guidance for technology and media examples
- Marking guide and answer keys where appropriate
- QA bundle confirming no student shaming, forced online-habit disclosure, or manipulative-design objective

## Safety Controls

Cycle F must avoid shaming students for technology use, turning media psychology into moral panic, or teaching manipulation as a goal. Tasks should emphasize evidence, design awareness, autonomy, ethics, and responsible critique.
