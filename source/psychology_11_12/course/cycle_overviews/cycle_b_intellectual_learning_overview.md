# Cycle B Overview: Intellectual / Learning

## Core Question

How do humans learn, think, create, and adapt?

## Primary Course Function

Cycle B studies learning, memory, cognition, motivation, creativity, intelligence, education, and applied skill development. It turns the evidence habits from Cycle A toward how people build skill, solve problems, and adapt across contexts.

## Module Sequence

| Module | Focus | Major Domains | Student Work | Safety / QA Control |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| B1 | Brain, attention, and learning | Brain/body; attention; sleep; neuroplasticity. | Explain how a fictional learner improves a skill. | Avoid fixed-brain or deficit framing. |
| B2 | Learning theory | Classical conditioning; operant conditioning; reinforcement; feedback. | Build a practice plan for a fictional skill. | Avoid manipulative behaviour-control framing. |
| B3 | Memory and study | Encoding; retrieval; working memory; long-term memory; forgetting. | Compare weak and strong study strategies. | Avoid public ranking of student ability. |
| B4 | Cognition, intelligence, creativity | Problem-solving; bias; creativity; intelligence. | Creativity/problem-solving challenge with evidence reflection. | Treat intelligence testing critically and carefully. |
| B5 | Motivation and development | Executive function; adolescence; persistence; achievement. | Case study of an adolescent learner and support design. | Avoid deficit framing. |
| B6 | Learning systems and equity | Peer effects; teacher expectations; stereotype threat; access. | Analyze how context affects a fictional learner. | Avoid putting students on the spot about identity, class, or ability. |

## Required Through-Line Prompts

| Through-Line | Prompt |
|---|---|
| Cultural / Contextual | How do access, expectations, language, culture, and school context shape learning? |
| Developmental / Lifespan | How do attention, executive function, motivation, and learning change across development? |
| Biological / Brain-Body | What role do attention, sleep, stress, arousal, practice, and neuroplasticity play? |
| Ethical / Evidence | What evidence supports this learning strategy, and where could advice become overconfident or unfair? |

## Capstone Frame

Design an evidence-based learning or performance support for a fictional person, team, class, or workplace. Students must explain brain/body, learning, memory, motivation, context, evidence, and ethics.

## Student-Facing Artifact List

- Cycle opener / lens overview
- Learning theory concept cards
- Memory and study comparison task
- Fictional learner case study
- Evidence-based practice plan
- Context/equity learning analysis
- Capstone packet

## Teacher-Facing Artifact List

- Teacher binder with pacing and delivery notes
- Misconception guide for learning, memory, and intelligence claims
- Assessment keys and marking guide
- Differentiation support for varied reading, memory, and executive-function needs
- QA bundle confirming student-facing artifacts do not rank, label, or expose students

## Safety Controls

Cycle B should emphasize agency and evidence without implying students are fixed by brain differences, intelligence labels, motivation levels, or learning profiles. Use fictional learners and neutral skill contexts rather than public ranking or personal disclosure.

## Active Source Status

Cycle B is an active external package not yet normalized into the lens-based architecture. It should be normalized after Cycle A.
