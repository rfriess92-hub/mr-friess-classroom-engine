# Cycle E Overview: Justice / Responsibility

## Core Question

How should psychology be used when harm, safety, memory, law, and accountability are involved?

## Primary Course Function

Cycle E maps legal and forensic applications of psychology with strong attention to evidence, memory reliability, bias, risk, responsibility, development, and ethics. It must stay academically bounded and must not drift into graphic true-crime entertainment.

## Module Sequence

| Module | Focus | Major Domains | Student Work | Safety / QA Control |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| E1 | Decision-making under stress | Brain/body; threat; arousal; impulse control. | Analyze decision-making under stress in a sanitized case. | Avoid graphic harm details. |
| E2 | Eyewitness memory | Memory; false memory; confidence; misinformation. | Eyewitness reliability case analysis. | Use sanitized or fictional cases. |
| E3 | Bias and legal judgment | Cognition; attribution; stereotyping; moral reasoning. | Legal judgment bias scenarios. | Avoid inflammatory real-world debates unless carefully framed. |
| E4 | Authority, conformity, and group decisions | Social psychology; authority; obedience; conformity; jury dynamics. | Fictional low-risk jury deliberation simulation. | Avoid true-crime entertainment. |
| E5 | Development, risk, and responsibility | Adolescent development; risk-taking; accountability; rehabilitation. | Case study on adolescent responsibility. | Avoid deterministic language about youth. |
| E6 | Mental health, law, and ethics | Competency; responsibility; stigma; expert testimony; confidentiality. | Forensic ethics board review. | No diagnosis of real people. |

## Required Through-Line Prompts

| Through-Line | Prompt |
|---|---|
| Cultural / Contextual | How do power, authority, bias, institutions, community context, and access affect judgment and responsibility? |
| Developmental / Lifespan | How might age, adolescence, development, rehabilitation, and responsibility interact? |
| Biological / Brain-Body | What role do threat, arousal, stress, impulse control, and regulation play, and what should psychology not overclaim? |
| Ethical / Evidence | What can psychology responsibly conclude in legal contexts, and what are the risks of misuse? |

## Capstone Frame

Analyze a sanitized fictional legal/forensic case file. Students explain what psychology can and cannot responsibly conclude using memory, cognition, social influence, development, evidence, and ethics.

## Student-Facing Artifact List

- Cycle opener / lens overview
- Sanitized case-file packet
- Eyewitness reliability task
- Legal judgment bias scenarios
- Authority/conformity group-decision task
- Forensic ethics board review
- Capstone packet

## Teacher-Facing Artifact List

- Teacher binder with true-crime drift controls
- Sanitized case selection guidance
- Misconception guide for memory, forensic psychology, and mental health/law claims
- Marking guide and answer keys where appropriate
- QA bundle confirming no graphic harm, diagnosis of real people, or sensationalized crime framing

## Safety Controls

Cycle E must avoid graphic crime detail, sensationalism, amateur profiling, diagnosing real people, and reducing responsibility to one psychological explanation. Legal/forensic examples should be sanitized, fictional, or carefully bounded public examples.
