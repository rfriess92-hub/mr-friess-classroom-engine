# Cycle D Overview: Wellness / Change

## Core Question

How do people cope, struggle, change, and access support?

## Primary Course Function

Cycle D studies stress, health, coping, mental health literacy, treatment awareness, resilience, support systems, and responsible help-seeking. It has the highest safety-control requirement because students may connect the content to private distress, diagnosis, trauma, or family experiences.

## Module Sequence

| Module | Focus | Major Domains | Student Work | Safety / QA Control |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| D1 | Stress and the body | Stress response; nervous system; endocrine system; sleep; health. | Explain the stress response in a fictional scenario. | Avoid trauma disclosure. |
| D2 | Habits, avoidance, and coping | Learning; reinforcement; avoidance; coping patterns. | Behaviour-change plan for a fictional low-risk habit. | Do not ask students to disclose personal struggles. |
| D3 | Appraisal and rumination | Cognition; problem-solving; appraisal; thought patterns. | Compare helpful and unhelpful coping thoughts in scenarios. | Keep examples mild and fictional. |
| D4 | Emotion regulation and resilience | Emotion; motivation; support; resilience. | Emotion-regulation strategy analysis. | Do not make it therapy practice. |
| D5 | Mental health literacy | Distress vs disorder; diagnosis boundaries; stigma; impairment. | Myth-busting source analysis. | No self-diagnosis or peer diagnosis. |
| D6 | Treatment and support systems | Therapy approaches; medication literacy; school/community supports; referral pathways. | Support-awareness resource. | No crisis role-play or peer counselling. |

## Required Through-Line Prompts

| Through-Line | Prompt |
|---|---|
| Cultural / Contextual | How do stigma, access, community, family expectations, language, and systems shape support? |
| Developmental / Lifespan | How do stress, coping, risk, protective factors, and help-seeking change across development? |
| Biological / Brain-Body | What nervous system, endocrine, sleep, stress, or health processes are involved, and what are the limits of biology-only explanations? |
| Ethical / Evidence | What does evidence support, what are the limits, and what should students not try to diagnose or treat? |

## Capstone Frame

Create a mental health literacy or support-awareness product, or analyze a fictional case with clear boundaries: concept accuracy, stigma reduction, support literacy, and appropriate referral awareness.

## Student-Facing Artifact List

- Cycle opener / lens overview
- Fictional stress-response scenario
- Coping and habit-analysis organizer
- Appraisal/rumination scenario comparison
- Mental health literacy myth audit
- Support-awareness resource task
- Capstone packet

## Teacher-Facing Artifact List

- Teacher binder with high-control safety notes
- Disclosure-boundary and referral-awareness guidance
- Misconception guide for diagnosis, treatment, and support language
- Marking guide and answer keys where appropriate
- QA bundle confirming no therapy role-play, crisis simulation, diagnosis task, or forced private disclosure

## Safety Controls

Cycle D must not ask students to disclose diagnoses, trauma, self-harm history, family mental health history, therapy experiences, medication use, or personal distress. The course may teach mental health literacy and support awareness, but it must not train students to diagnose, counsel, or act as peer therapists.

## Active Source Status

Cycle D is an active external package not yet normalized into the lens-based architecture. It should be normalized after Cycle C.
